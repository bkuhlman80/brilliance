# # Notes on Personality

*Informational note. Constitution Part III (§01.08–§01.14) specifies the activation architecture; Part IV (§01.15–§01.20) specifies the integration architecture and the two-coordinate disorder mapping. The April 2026 reframe established activation and integration as two parallel readings of one Stack, not two labels for the same construct. This Note preserves the Pan evidence, the detailed disorder mapping, the Worldliness mechanism developed in prior drafts, and the structural observations that fall out of the two-coordinate view.*

## Activation and integration

The project’s April 2026 reframe treats activation and integration as two different kinds of architectural stuff, not two labels for the same construct. Activation describes *what the organism does* — bipolar intensities of gambling policy across composite domains defined by the four basic motivations, mapped by the Venn geometry. Integration describes *how well what the organism does hangs together* — supervisory binding quality between Stack layers, structured as a cascade of interfaces (V↔B, M↔earlier, A↔earlier, H↔earlier). The two vary independently. Same activation profile plus different integration quality produces a different phenotype; same integration quality plus different activation profile produces a different personality style within the same integration band. This Note is organized accordingly: activation architecture first, integration second, disorder mapping (which uses both as coordinates) last.

## The activation architecture

Constitution §01.10–§01.13 specifies the Venn geometry: four basic motivations, six pairwise intersections, four triples, Appetite for Action as the four-way center, emergence sequence across the Stack. This section preserves the empirical case that the Venn is doing real work — that its regions are population-differentiable structural locations rather than arbitrary labels.

### The Pan personality puzzle

The same 54-item Hominoid Personality Questionnaire administered across chimpanzee and bonobo populations produces six factors every time — but never the same six. Which factor coheres and which dissolves tracks the ecology of the population, not just sample size.

**Dominance** emerges cleanly in zoo chimps, lab chimps, and wild chimps. It is the most stable chimpanzee factor. In bonobos it is relabeled Assertiveness and its composition shifts — top loaders become reversed anxiety, timidity, and fearfulness. In wild bonobos no clear Dominance/Assertiveness factor emerged (though N=16 is too small for reliable factor analysis). The sex difference reverses: males higher in chimps, females higher in bonobos.

**Neuroticism** emerges in zoo chimps but not lab chimps (where its items merge into Reactivity/Undependability). It does not emerge in bonobos at all. The items that define chimp Neuroticism (anxious, fearful, vulnerable, excitable) load as reversed Assertiveness in bonobos. The trait content is present and varies. The factor does not cohere.

**Attentiveness** emerges only in bonobos. Defining items: intelligent (.68), not disorganized (−.71), not clumsy (−.68), not thoughtless (−.68), not distractible (−.59). Congruence coefficients against all three chimp samples uniformly below .85. In chimps, these items scatter across Dominance and Conscientiousness.

**Conscientiousness, Openness, and Agreeableness** are the stable core across both species.

### How ecology tunes the four systems

Constitution §01.14 states the resolution: the Venn architecture is fixed; what varies is which basic motivation’s boundary is sharpened by ecological pressure, which differentiates the complementary triple (the one missing that basic motivation) from the center. The counterintuitive part: high Mastery pressure does *not* sharpen Conscientiousness. It sharpens **Dominant** (the triple missing Mastery), because Mastery pulling clean away from the center gives the remaining three-motivation blend its own factor-analytic identity. Full ecology-to-basic-motivation-to-triple mappings with Pan evidence are tabulated in Constitution §01.14.

The same four ecology → basic-motivation-sharpened pairings correspond to the four Strauss-Howe generational archetypes (Constitution §01.23; Notes on Civilization). This is a cross-scale parallel: the mechanism that differentiates Pan factor structure by population is the same mechanism that differentiates cohort personality signatures by generation. The ecological pressure in each case is different (coalitionary competition for Reactive Nomads; prosocial coordination for Civic Heroes; frustrated autonomy for Adaptive Artists; high-risk signaling for Idealistic Prophets), but the architectural move is identical — sharpen one basic motivation, differentiate the complementary triple.

### Biology of the chimp-bonobo difference

**Genetic architecture.** Bonobos entirely lack the AVPR1A DupB deletion polymorphic in chimps. Bonobos show 57% more fixed substitutions in four neurochemical receptor genes (AVPR1A, OXTR, DRD2, HTR1A); chimps show 31% more polymorphic coding variation. More fixed substitutions in bonobos suggests stronger directional selection — consistent with Affiliation-sharpening. More polymorphic variation in chimps suggests maintained diversity — consistent with frequency-dependent selection under Mastery-sharpened ecology.

**Hormonal profiles.** Bonobo males respond to feeding competition with cortisol (stress framing); chimp males respond with testosterone (dominance framing). Chimps show a clear developmental testosterone rise; bonobos show minimal pubertal increases. Bonobos retain juvenile play levels into adulthood.

**Neuroanatomy.** Bonobo amygdalae contain more than twice the density of serotonin transporter-immunoreactive axons compared to chimps, with more gray matter in empathy-related regions (right dorsal amygdala, right anterior insula, ventral ACC) and a larger amygdala-to-ACC pathway.

These three biological lines converge on the same architectural claim: bonobos and chimps have the same four-basic-motivation structure but live under ecologies that sharpen different basic motivations, with measurable differences in the neural and endocrine substrate that supports the sharpened basic motivation.

### The Deceptive factor and the symbolic threshold

The Deceptive factor is the one corner of the Pan resolution pattern without direct empirical confirmation — no Pan study has identified a clean Deceptive factor despite the genus being the highest-scoring primate taxon for tactical deception. The project’s reading is that this is architecturally expected, not an embarrassment.

Deacon argues that symbols are not required for Machiavellian cleverness — indexical communication is sufficient for complex social relationships. The Deceptive factor at the ape level (System 2) operates indexically: tactical deception references things that are perceptually present. Deacon further argues that the transition to symbolic thought was driven by the need to represent social contracts — abstract commitments and future obligations that cannot be seen or pointed to.

On this reading, the Deceptive factor occupies the structural position from which the symbolic threshold is crossed. At the human level, the same Mastery × Affiliation × Autonomy intersection transforms from Machiavellian tactical deception into symbolic legislation — moral codes, abstract obligations, social contracts. What Pan lacks is not the activation region but the symbolic machinery that turns the region into something factor-analytically distinct. The Deceptive factor may not cohere in Pan populations precisely because the symbolic supervision that would give it its characteristic content has not yet arrived.

### Cross-cultural instability of Neuroticism and Openness

Two Big Five constructs misbehave cross-culturally in ways the architecture predicts.

**Neuroticism** is a triple intersection (B × M × Af, absent Autonomy). All four triples require coordination of three systems and have more degrees of freedom in how the components combine than pairwise intersections do. Different cultural environments may weight the components differently, producing factor structures where Neuroticism conflates with Dominance, Impulsivity, or Deceptiveness. The instability isn’t measurement error — it reflects genuine architectural complexity. This is also the architectural reason Neuroticism keeps factor-analytically misbehaving as a higher-order construct rather than a clean basic motivation (Constitution §01.10).

**Openness/Intellect** faces an additional instability source: the Open aspect (System 2, ape-level) and the Intellect aspect (System 3, human-level) are architecturally layered rather than blended. Different cultures and instruments may tap one layer more than the other, producing different factor structures depending on which layer dominates the item content.

## The integration architecture

Constitution §01.15–§01.17 specifies integration as a Bennett-style accretion of supervisory interfaces — V↔B, M↔(earlier), A↔(earlier), H↔(earlier) — each being the relationship by which a later Stack layer modulates an earlier one. This section preserves the argumentation behind Worldliness as the H↔(earlier) interface construct, the MGR mechanism, and the predicted convergence between Stability meta-trait and Common EF.

### Worldliness

Constitution §01.19 positions Worldliness as a candidate construct at the H↔(earlier) integration interface. The prior framing — Worldliness as the four-way activation center — was an error and has been retired. Appetite for Action is the four-way activation center (Constitution §01.12); Worldliness sits on the integration architecture.

The H↔(earlier) interface requires all four ape-level basic motivations to be activated and coordinated, because symbolic supervision supervenes on the full ape-level activation base. This is the repositioned form of the “Worldliness requires all four systems” argument from earlier drafts. The argument’s structure survives; its architectural position has moved from activation center to integration interface.

- **Boldness** — willingness to engage with the symbolic environment outside the family
- **Mastery** — competence at absorbing and performing the generation’s skills and norms
- **Affiliation** — bonding with age-peers and absorbing their social conventions
- **Autonomy** — sufficient self-governance to navigate the symbolic environment independently. Without this, you’re compliant, not worldly.

Remove any one and what remains is not Worldliness but something simpler: without Boldness → withdrawn from generational culture; without Mastery → exposed but unable to perform the generational style; without Affiliation → intellectually engaged but socially disconnected from peers; without Autonomy → absorbing family culture rather than generational culture. The H-level symbolic machinery has nothing to supervise unless the ape-level substrate is fully online.

At the ape level, the corresponding integration construct is Domesticated (Constitution §01.16) — the self-domestication phenotype in which the four ape-level basic motivations bind coherently under social-cognitive regulation. Worldliness at the human level is the same kind of thing one Stack level up: binding quality at the supervisory interface, not intensity of activation. Domesticated and Worldliness are plausibly two manifestations of a single construct — working hypothesis under development is that they together correspond to DeYoung’s Plasticity meta-trait, paralleling the Common EF / Stability convergence discussed below at a different cascade level.

Whether Worldliness survives long-term as the canonical H-level integration construct or gets refined/replaced by a sharper construct (for instance, a refinement of Stability meta-trait extended to symbolic supervision) is flagged as open (Constitution §02.12).

### How Worldliness gets set: the System 3 vs System 0–2 channels

The mechanism by which Worldliness gets set during development is the Meme-to-Gene Ratio (Constitution §01.19): time in meme-carrying activities divided by time in gene-carrying activities during the formative window (~ages 10–22). The numerator is System 3 content from the cohort; the denominator is System 0–2 content from the family and solo time.

The sorting principle for which activities go into which side of the ratio: **does this activity expose you to other people’s models of how to be?** Activities at the high end of the numerator (peer interaction, solo media consumption, social media) carry the generational signal. Activities at the low end (solitary non-media time, family-only time) carry the family signal or allow individual temperament to express without external override.

The inheritance gradient (Constitution §01.18) predicts that cultural transmission increasingly competes with genetic and parental transmission as you move up the Stack. High Worldliness at the H↔(earlier) interface is what it looks like when the System 3 channel wins the competition against the System 0–2 channels during the formative window.

### Stability meta-trait and Common EF

Constitution §01.17 identifies several constructs that may be measuring the integration architecture at different levels of description — Stability meta-trait at the personality-trait level, Common EF at the cognitive-mechanism level, p-factor as a partial index of endotherm- and primate-level integration failure. The project’s central prediction about these constructs: **Stability meta-trait and Common EF are the same underlying integration construct measured at different levels of description.**

The empirical anchors available so far:

- **Stability (Lewis 2016).** Stability predicted uncinate fasciculus fractional anisotropy (β = 0.27). After partialling Stability, the residuals for Agreeableness, Conscientiousness, and (low) Neuroticism showed zero independent association with uncinate FA. This is the cleanest existing evidence that Stability is capturing integrative white-matter infrastructure rather than anything specific to any of the Big Five constructs it aggregates.
- **Common EF (Friedman & Miyake literature; Hatoum 2023).** Shared variance across executive function tasks (inhibition, updating, shifting) has a GABAergic substrate and retains transdiagnostic predictive power after IQ control.

The convergence prediction implies that Stability and Common EF, when both are properly modeled in the same sample after partialling out activation-level variance, should show strong convergence. **This test has not been performed.** The deep-research reconnaissance flagged it as the single highest-value empirical gap in the activation-versus-integration literature.

If the prediction holds, the integration architecture has a clean multi-level structure: the same dimension measured by Stability at the personality level, Common EF at the cognitive-capacity level, and eventually by neural integration substrates (GABAergic interneuron function, MD thalamus hub integration, white-matter tract integrity) at the mechanism level. If it fails, the integration architecture may be multi-dimensional rather than one dimension with multiple measurements. Either outcome informs the project.

## Disorder mapping

### Each disorder gets an activation and an integration read

Constitution §01.20 specifies that a disorder gets two coordinates:

- **Activation coordinate** — where on the Venn: which basic motivations dominate, which intersections activate, with what intensity.
- **Integration coordinate** — which supervisory interface in the cascade is weakest.

The single-coordinate mapping in earlier drafts of the project (which read “Venn region → disorder”) is superseded. What an activation-region-only map tells you is the *personality style* associated with a region. Whether that style manifests as functional behavior or as clinical disorder depends on the integration coordinate: which supervisory link, if any, is weak. This is why the same activation profile can produce successful or unsuccessful phenotypes (developed below).

The diagnostic logic is *weakest supervisory link in the stack*. Each integration interface has its own mechanism and its own failure mode; pathology appears at whichever link is weak. Three principles from Constitution §01.20 organize the space:

1. A disorder can only exist at a phylogenetic level if the architectural region it occupies is available at that level.
2. No basic-motivation extreme is a true disorder. Disorders live at intersections, triples, the center, and integration interfaces.
3. The internalizing/externalizing divide *is* the Autonomy divide. Internalizing (Neurotic triple) is the only triple lacking Autonomy; all three externalizing spectra live on Autonomy-bearing triples.

The tables that follow are the activation-coordinate mapping. The integration coordinate sits in parallel and is discussed in its own right after the tables.

### Six observations from the disorder mapping

Six observations fall out of the two-coordinate mapping.

**Externalizing is an ape invention.** No externalizing pathology exists before Autonomy arrives. Non-primate mammals can suffer (the Neurotic triple is available at the endotherm level) but cannot exploit (the three Dark Triad triples all require Autonomy and so are ape-level and above). Non-primate mammalian “personality disorders” should therefore all be internalizing-like or basic-motivation-extreme-like — never Dark-Triad-like.

**Dominant transforms across levels.** Endotherm Dominant is B×Af pairwise: reactive social aggression (hot, approach-oriented, unsophisticated). Ape Dominant is the B×Af×Au triple absent M: narcissistic exploitation (cold, strategic, reputation-obsessed). An aggressive dog and a narcissistic chimpanzee are both “dominant” in some sense, but the mechanisms are structurally different. The Constitution §01.13 notes this as the Dominant transformation: what changes between levels is what Autonomy adds, not what it removes.

**Mania and anhedonia are architectural inverses.** Both sit on the Extraverted pairwise (Af×Au). Bipolar cycling is oscillation between extremes on the same architectural region. This is the architectural reason bipolar disorder cycles specifically between high-energy hypersocial states and low-energy anhedonic states, rather than between some other pair of opposites.

**BPD is an attachment disorder, not a Machiavellian one.** Borderline sits at extreme high Agreeable (M×Af), not at the Deceptive triple. The “manipulation” historically attributed to BPD is desperate attachment behavior amplified by emotional dysregulation, not calculated strategic deception. The Deceptive triple (M×Af×Au) requires Autonomy that Borderline presentations typically do not have in the strategic sense.

**Dissociation is self-directed deception.** The Deceptive triple’s clinical expression (dissociative disorders) and its normal-range expression (Machiavellianism) look phenomenologically opposite. The architectural link: both involve information management without Boldness to confront material directly. Machiavellianism directs deception outward; dissociation directs it inward.

**The veterinary medicine test.** Non-primate mammalian behavioral pathology should be overwhelmingly internalizing-like: separation anxiety, compulsive disorders, phobias (Neurotic triple and high Conscientious). Reactive aggression maps to endotherm Dominant pairwise (B×Af). The list of pathologies *absent* in non-primate mammals should include narcissistic manipulation, strategic deception, mania, thought disorder, and dissociation. Veterinary clinical literature provides the empirical check.

### Successful vs unsuccessful psychopathy

The cleanest worked example of the two-coordinate logic is the successful-vs-unsuccessful psychopathy dissociation (Yang 2005; Ishikawa 2001).

Both variants share the same activation coordinate: the Impulsive triple (B×M×Au, absent Affiliation), with D-led Appetite for Action — bold, competent, self-directed, unable or unwilling to bond. On the activation map alone, they are the same phenotype. But they are clinically and socially very different: successful psychopaths are high-functioning and often unconvicted; unsuccessful psychopaths are repeatedly incarcerated and disastrous in their own lives.

The difference is the integration coordinate. Successful psychopaths have intact A↔(earlier) integration — mentalizing and self-directed agency are functioning, which means the exploitative activation profile is deployed strategically with anticipation of consequences. Unsuccessful psychopaths have weak A↔(earlier) integration on the same activation profile — mentalizing is impaired, consequences go unanticipated, the exploitative behavior is reactive rather than strategic.

This is the general pattern: activation coordinate predicts personality style; integration coordinate predicts whether the style produces functional or disordered behavior. The same logic applies across the disorder space — same Venn region, different weak link, different clinical phenotype. It is also why single-coordinate diagnostic systems keep fragmenting under cross-validation: they are aggregating phenotypes that share an activation coordinate while differing in their integration coordinate, or vice versa.
