## §01 — Canonical Architecture

This section reorganizes around what the project is now committed to: **two kinds of mental stuff** (activation traits and integration machinery) inhabiting **one 3D structure** (a phylogenetic stack viewable from two angles). The old Constitution treated the Venn diagram as the single architectural object; this version treats activation and integration as separate, parallel, and equally real.

The architecture extends from organism to civilization. The civilization-level extension is structurally analogous and methodologically continuous, but it is a smaller part of the project than the organism-level architecture, and its claims carry higher uncertainty.

# Part I — Claim Certainty

This Part comes first because everything that follows is at stake. The architecture generates predictions it does not have to make. Stating in advance which claims are exposed, and to what, prevents the failure of an ambitious prediction from contaminating the foundations it was built on.

### 01.01 Claim Certainty

The project’s claims sort into three tiers by exposure to empirical disconfirmation. **Disconfirmation propagates upward, not downward.** Failure of a Tier 3 claim does not impeach Tier 1 or Tier 2. Failure of Tier 1 brings everything down with it.

#### Tier 1 — Foundational Architecture

**Claims.** The Stack (§01.03). Allostatic regulation as the foundation (§01.02). The four basic motivations and their derivation from Bennett’s breakthroughs in evolution (§01.08). The activation architecture and its nested-trait structure (§01.10–§01.11). Activation emergence and the differentiation pattern (§01.13). Agency and Warmth as computational modes (§01.09). The tries-to-explain hierarchy (§01.06). The seven life tasks as the selection-pressure context (§01.05).

**Evidence base.** Comparative psychology, phylogenetic constraints (architectural traits cannot exist before their substrates evolve), neuroanatomical layering (Bennett’s staircase), disorder availability by phylogenetic level (§01.20).

**Falsified by.** Demonstrating Autonomy-like strategic personality structure in a non-primate mammal. Showing the Dark Triad with the same cold/strategic character in a species lacking granular PFC. Finding an endotherm population where internalizing-like pathology is absent. Showing that the Neurotic triple emerges in ectotherms.

**Risk level.** Low. These claims are constrained by deep comparative evidence and make retrodictions that are already checkable.

#### Tier 2 — Architectural Consequences at the Human Level

**Claims.** Appetite for Action as the derived trait at ape and human levels, with four faces (D, A, Z, F) (§01.12). The integration architecture: supervisory interfaces between activation cascades, with weakest-link logic for pathology (§01.15). Integration accretion in parallel with activation emergence (§01.16). The Pan personality puzzle and its resolution by ecology-driven boundary sharpening (§01.14). The two-coordinate disorder mapping: same activation profile + different integration weak link = different disorder (§01.20). Worldliness as the H↔earlier integration interface, mechanized by the meme-to-gene ratio (§01.19). The inheritance gradient (§01.18).

**Evidence base.** Pan population comparisons (Factor Whac-a-Mole). HiTOP structural alignment. Schreiber & Marcus (2020) meta-analytic establishment of D as orthogonal to Stability and Plasticity. Lewis (2016) on Stability ↔ uncinate fasciculus integrity. Friedman & Miyake’s Common EF literature. Successful vs. unsuccessful psychopathy dissociation (Yang 2005, Ishikawa 2001). Danchin’s inclusive heritability and Turkheimer’s phenotypic null.

**Falsified by.** Showing that the Common EF / Stability convergence does not survive proper measurement and partialling. Showing that ecological pressure does not predict which Pan personality factors differentiate. Demonstrating that the Pan puzzle’s predictions (high mastery pressure sharpens Dominant, not Conscientiousness) fail systematically across populations. Showing that successful and unsuccessful psychopaths differ in activation profile rather than in integration coordinate.

**Risk level.** Moderate. These claims follow from Tier 1 architecture applied to human and Pan data, but the empirical tests are harder to run and the measurement infrastructure is still developing.

#### Tier 3 — Speculative Dynamics

**Claims.** The four-phase generational cycle and its rotation around the ring topology (§01.23). The variance predictions: high meme-to-gene ratio should increase between-cohort variability and decrease between-family variability. The civilization-level activation pools and historical emergence timeline (§01.21). The civilization-level integration cascades operating at three timescales (§01.22). The substrate-inversion hypothesis: digital technology degrades the experiential cascade by making forgetting effortful and remembering default. The globalization-as-frontier-dissolution hypothesis: degradation of meta-ethnic frontiers reduces between-civilization selection pressure that maintains internal coordination.

**Evidence base.** Thin. The meme-to-gene ratio is operationally defined but not yet measured. The generational cycle is pattern-matched to Strauss-Howe and to Turchin’s structural-demographic data; the long-cycle component is empirically untested within current data windows and requires ~160+ years of consistent cross-cohort measurement to distinguish from trend-only alternatives (§01.23). The variance predictions have not been tested. The civilization-level architecture is at the speculative edge of the project.

**What Tier 3 is.** The architecture, taken seriously as a dynamical system, applied to questions where most personality theories go silent. These are pre-registered bets about patterns no one has yet looked for, generated by the framework’s internal logic. The theory earns the right to make them by specifying in advance what would count as disconfirmation.

**Falsified by.** Demonstrating that within-cohort personality variance is stable across pre-digital and post-digital cohorts (at the same age, with measurement invariance established). Showing that the meme-to-gene ratio, once operationalized and measured, does not predict Worldliness. Showing that generational personality shifts do not rotate around the ring topology in the predicted order. Showing that globalization-era societies do not show the predicted asabiyya proxies decline.

**Risk level.** High. These are where the project is most exposed and most original.

#### The Firewall: One-Directional Disconfirmation

Tier 3 is built on Tier 2, which is built on Tier 1. The dependency is one-directional. If the generational cycle turns out to be wrong, this tells you the architecture’s dynamical predictions at the civilizational scale were incorrect. It does not tell you the four basic motivations are wrong. The Stack describes how personality is built. The cycle describes how it might oscillate across cohorts. These are different claims with different evidence bases.

A personality theory that explains everything below the human level and goes quiet about generational dynamics is a theory that has stopped early. The cost of stopping early is paid in explanatory ambition. The cost of going further is paid in falsifiable bets the project agrees in advance to lose if the data come back negative.

# Part II — Foundations

This Part establishes what the rest of the architecture is built on: the regulatory logic (allostatic gambling), the phylogenetic structure (the Stack), the relationship between the two kinds of mental stuff (activation and integration), the selection-pressure context (the seven life tasks), the predictive hierarchy (tries-to-explain), and the conceptual hazards readers bring with them (the Traps).

### 01.02 Allostasis and Metabolic Gambling

The organism does not defend fixed values. It predicts what it will need and adjusts in advance. This is **allostasis** — distinguished from homeostasis (set-point defense) by three converging properties:

1. **Predictive.** Adjusts before the need arises, based on cues that are not the need itself.
2. **Flexible.** The prediction updates from the organism’s own experience, not solely from hardwired programs.
3. **Set-point-adjusting.** The defended value itself changes as a result of experience.

When all three converge, the system is genuinely allostatic. It can also fail in ways a thermostat cannot: defending wrong values, anticipating threats that are not coming, redefining goals in directions that damage it. Human obesity is allostatic overload — the brain chronically anticipates higher energy reserves, recalibrates appetite upward, defends the new configuration as if it were correct.

**Allostasis is substrate-independent.** Slime molds (Physarum) and plant guard cells (Arabidopsis) meet all three criteria with no neurons. Brains do not invent allostasis — they make it dramatically more powerful. This matters because it places the regulatory logic upstream of the nervous system: the Stack is the story of how allostatic regulation gets progressively more sophisticated, not how it gets invented.

**Personality is a metabolic gambling policy.** The organism has a finite metabolic budget and faces uncertainty about what behaviors will pay off. Personality is the convergent style of bet the organism makes — the policy it has settled on for which gambles to take and which to skip. Every architectural construct in the project, from individual basic motivations to civilization-level pools, is a class of bet over the organism’s resources under uncertainty.

The fast-slow life-history-trade-off framing that previously anchored the vertebrate level is retired as causal explanation. Genetic correlations between survival and reproduction run positive, not negative (Chang et al. 2024); the fitness landscape across natural clutch size variation is essentially flat (Bliard et al. 2025); the pace-of-life syndrome has failed to generalize across taxa (Laskowski et al. 2022; Dochtermann 2023). The phenomenology survives — bold-fast-aggressive clustering is real — but the explanation shifts from allocation-from-a-fixed-pool to calibration-of-gambling-style-to-ecology.

### 01.03 The Stack

The phylogenetic sequence on which everything else is built. Each level adds a new computational primitive that elaborates the regulatory machinery of the levels below.

|Level        |System|Computational Breakthrough|Substrate                                                                    |What Emerges                                                                         |
|-------------|------|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Cell         |0     |Self-maintenance          |Biochemical signaling, gene regulation                                       |Allostatic foundation                                                                |
|Multicellular|0.1   |Coordination across parts |Cell-cell communication, immune system, hormones                             |First superorganism (body as cell colony)                                            |
|Bilaterian   |0.2   |Steering                  |First centralized nervous systems, monoamines, reflex arcs                   |Affect (valence + arousal). First basic motivation: **Boldness**                     |
|Vertebrate   |0.3   |Reinforcement             |Basal ganglia, HPA axis, amygdala, hippocampus, first telencephalon          |Model-free RL. Five life tasks. Second basic motivation: **Mastery**                 |
|Endotherm    |1     |Simulation (1st-order)    |Agranular PFC ↔ sensory neocortex (mammals); pallium (birds, convergent)     |Model-based RL. Attachment. Third basic motivation: **Affiliation**                  |
|Ape          |2     |Mentalizing (2nd-order)   |Granular PFC ↔ STS & TPJ                                                     |Models of other minds. Fourth basic motivation: **Autonomy**                         |
|Human        |3     |Symbolic compression      |Languages, writing, institutions, internet. Wernicke’s + Broca’s as interface|Arbitrary semiotics. Cumulative culture. **No new basic motivation.** Integration cascade only|

The bracketed terms in parentheses (Bennett, Deacon) credit the theoretical sources: Max Bennett’s *A Brief History of Intelligence* for the sequence of breakthroughs in evolution and Terrence Deacon’s *Symbolic Species* for the human-level integration cascade.

**Phase transitions** occur at integer boundaries: 0→1 (reactive → simulative), 1→2 (modeling world → modeling minds), 2→3 (inside skulls → between and beyond them).

**Activation caps at the ape level.** The human level adds no new activation pool. Following Bennett and Deacon, the brain differences between apes and humans are modest (elaborated Wernicke’s, Broca’s); the qualitative difference is symbolic processing, which is integration machinery, not new activation. Symbolic compression elaborates the binding, transmission, and supervision of the four ape-level basic motivations; it does not add a fifth.

**Dual reading.** The Stack is one structure read from two angles. From above (top-down), each level contributes to the Venn diagram of activation traits (§01.10). From the side, the cascades are arrows running upward — supervisory interfaces by which later layers modulate earlier ones (§01.15). See §01.04.

### 01.04 Activation and Integration

The project is committed to two kinds of mental stuff occupying one 3D structure.

**Activation traits** are *what the organism does*. They are bipolar individual-difference dimensions describing how intensely the organism runs gambling policies in composite configurations defined by the four basic motivations. The Venn diagram (§01.10) is the top-down view of these traits.

**Integration machinery** is *how well what the organism does hangs together*. It is the supervisory architecture by which later cascades modulate earlier ones, binding distributed multi-system activity into coherent states. Integration is not organized as a set of activation traits. It is a Bennett-style accretion of supervisory interfaces (§01.15).

Activation and integration are orthogonal dimensions with independent phylogenetic schedules. Activation layers are the nodes; integration layers are the arrows between them. The orthogonality is empirical (Schreiber & Marcus 2020 establish D as orthogonal to the Stability and Plasticity meta-traits) as well as architectural.

**The 3D picture.** Imagine the Venn diagram rendered from directly above (top-down view): four overlapping circles, six pairwise traits, four triple traits, one derived trait. Now imagine rotating the view ninety degrees: you see the cascade structure running vertically — each Bennett layer is a horizontal stratum, with arrows running upward representing the supervisory interfaces of one layer over the layers below. The Venn diagram is the top-down silhouette of activation emergence. The cascade-and-interface structure is the side view of integration accretion. Same object, two angles.

Both kinds of stuff vary independently. Same activation profile + different integration quality = different phenotype. Same integration quality + different activation profile = different personality style within the same integration band. This independence is the architectural source of dissociations like successful vs. unsuccessful psychopathy (§01.20).

**Terminology note.** The project previously called these “axes.” That language is retired because it imports a misleading geometric metaphor — they are not perpendicular dimensions of one space, they are different categories of thing inhabiting one structure. The terms “activation” and “integration” stand alone as modifiers (activation construct, integration construct, activation emergence, integration accretion) without “axis.”

### 01.05 The Seven Life Tasks

After Kenrick et al. 2010, with base level redefined as allostasis. Life tasks are the selection-pressure context that produced the architecture. They are the adaptive problems the organism evolved to solve. They are not architectural traits and they do not map 1:1 onto activation traits (§02.15).

1. Allostasis (dynamic self-regulation)
2. Self-Protection
3. Affiliation (vertebrate-minimal: proximity regulation, not bonding)
4. Status / Esteem
5. Mate Acquisition
6. Mate Retention *(endotherm-only)*
7. Parenting *(endotherm-only)*

The first five are vertebrate-universal. The last two require endothermy and attachment-bonding, both of which arrive at the endotherm level. Life tasks accrete with the Stack but on a different combinatorial pattern from activation traits: there are 5 tasks at vertebrate level but only 3 activation traits; 7 tasks at endotherm level and (coincidentally) 7 activation traits; 7 tasks at ape level but 15 activation traits. The mismatch is informative — tasks are *what evolution had to solve*; **basic motivations** and their combinatorial geometry are *what evolution produced as the solution*. One **basic motivation** serves multiple tasks; one task recruits multiple **basic motivations**.

**Origin vs. deployment.** Basic motivations have task-specific evolutionary origins; their deployment is many-to-many. Affiliation’s oxytocin/vasopressin machinery arrived at endothermy under intensified Parenting and Mate Retention pressure (warm-blooded offspring require investment, which requires pair-bonding). Once Affiliation exists, it deploys across Self-Protection, Status, Affiliation-as-proximity, and more. Task-to-basic-motivation origin is narrower than task-to-basic-motivation deployment.

**Empirical prediction.** Task-specific personality instruments (parenting-style measures, romantic-attachment measures) should not cleanly isolate single activation traits. The factor-analytic messiness of domain-specific personality instruments is consistent with this — the measures are sampling across multiple basic motivations serving a single task.

### 01.06 The Tries-to-Explain Principle

Each Stack layer wraps around the layer below and generates predictions about it. This is Bennett’s predictive-processing architecture: each new computational primitive acts as a model of the activity of the lower layers.

|Layer                  |Hardware                            |Model Type                                                          |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
|System 2 (ape)         |gPFC ↔ STS & TPJ                    |2nd-order model (mind): tries to explain System 1’s intentions      |
|System 1 (endotherm)   |aPFC ↔ sensory cortex               |1st-order model (intent): tries to explain System 0’s reward signals|
|System 0.3 (vertebrate)|Basal ganglia, amygdala, hippocampus|Model-free learning from reflexes and sensory input                 |
|System 0.2 (bilaterian)|Reflex arcs                         |Direct sensory→motor connections                                    |

The principle continues at the human level: symbolic culture tries to explain collective behavior, detects prediction errors (this generation’s excesses), and generates corrections (next generation’s compensatory values). This is the mechanism that makes the generational cycle possible (§01.23).

**The “tries-to-explain” hierarchy is the activation reading of the Stack.** The integration reading of the same hierarchy is supervisory binding (§01.15) — what allows later-layer predictions to actually modulate earlier-layer activity rather than running in parallel and uncoordinated.

# Part III — Activation

This Part specifies the activation reading of the architecture: the four basic motivations, their combinatorial geometry, the derived trait at each stack level, the differentiation pattern across Stack levels, and the resolution of the Pan personality puzzle.

### 01.08 The Four Basic Motivations

|Basic Motivation|System          |Substrate                                      |Character                                  |
|----------------|----------------|-----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
|**Boldness**    |0.2 (Bilaterian)|Approach/avoid; reflex arcs, monoamines        |The most ancient personality trait         |
|**Mastery**    |0.3 (Vertebrate)|Reinforcement learning; basal ganglia, HPA axis|Gamification of the lifespan               |
|**Affiliation**|1 (Endotherm)   |Attachment; oxytocin/vasopressin, endothermy   |Warmth is literally warm-blooded           |
|**Autonomy**   |2 (Ape)         |Mentalizing, metacognition; granular PFC       |Machiavellian intelligence, self-governance|

**Ring order** (clockwise): Boldness – Mastery – Affiliation – Autonomy – (back to Boldness). Reflects phylogenetic emergence order. Determines which pairwise traits are ring-adjacent and which are diagonal (§01.10).

**Why exactly four.** The four basic motivations exist because Bennett’s pre-human stack has four pre-human accretion layers, each contributing one. The number is a structural consequence of the stack, not a theoretical posit. The human level adds no new basic motivation; activation caps at the ape level.

Each basic motivation is an activation construct: it measures how intensely the organism runs the corresponding gambling policy.

**Connection to existing personality psychology.** The four basic motivations map onto existing psychometric traditions in known ways, but the mapping is not 1:1 with Réale’s vertebrate temperament dimensions or DeYoung’s Big Five Aspects. The deeper architectural claim is that the activation architecture (§01.10) — pairwise traits, triple traits, the derived trait — *predicts* which constructs the field has discovered. See §01.10 for the full enumeration of how activation traits correspond to extant Big Five and beyond-Big-Five constructs.

**Connection to Self-Determination Theory.** Three of the four basic motivations — Mastery, Affiliation, Autonomy — correspond closely to Deci & Ryan’s basic psychological needs (competence, relatedness, autonomy). The fourth, Boldness, sits one Stack level below the others and does not register as a “need” in SDT’s frame; it is an ancient approach/avoid tendency that the human-scoped SDT literature did not have a vantage to catch. SDT and this project are doing the same work under different scope constraints: SDT asks what humans need to flourish; the Stack asks which action-tendency classes emerged at the Bennett breakthroughs in evolution. Where they overlap, they converge.

### 01.09 Agency and Warmth

Agency and warmth are not two different goals. They are two different computational strategies applied to the same goals.

- **Agency mode** (System 0): Direct behavioral action — I fight, I flee, I explore.
- **Warmth mode** (System 1): Representational/simulated engagement — I worry about safety, I maintain bonds.

Warmth requires simulation (System 1+). All System 0 personality is agency-only. This is why the warmth pole appears at the endotherm level and not before.

**Architectural consequence for vertebrate-level life tasks.** The first three life tasks (Allostasis, Self-Protection, Affiliation-as-proximity-regulation) are agency-only as a structural consequence, not an observational gap. Warmth-pole expression requires System 1 simulation machinery, which is not yet present at the vertebrate level.

### 01.10 The Activation Architecture

At the ape and human levels, all four circles overlap pairwise with all others, producing six pairwise traits. Each activation trait is a **bipolar activation dimension** describing how intensely the organism activates in the composite gambling configuration defined by the overlapping basic motivations. Every activation trait — pairwise, triple, and derived — is bipolar in this sense; no activation trait is intrinsically pathological at one pole or the other.

#### Six pairwise traits

|Trait                     |Components            |Ring Position|Character (activation dimension)                  |
|--------------------------|----------------------|-------------|--------------------------------------------------|
|**Conscientious**         |Boldness × Mastery    |Adjacent     |Disciplined goal-pursuit                          |
|**Agreeable**             |Mastery × Affiliation |Adjacent     |Competence directed toward social harmony         |
|**Extraverted**           |Affiliation × Autonomy|Adjacent     |Self-directed social engagement                   |
|**Open / Open-Intellect** |Autonomy × Boldness   |Adjacent     |Independent exploration + symbolic idea engagement|
|**Core Self-Concept**     |Boldness × Affiliation|Diagonal     |Self-evaluation in social context                 |
|**Strategic Self-Concept**|Autonomy × Mastery    |Diagonal     |Self-monitored competence display                 |

The two diagonal pairwise traits “skip” a system level in the tries-to-explain hierarchy: Boldness × Affiliation skips Mastery; Autonomy × Mastery skips Affiliation. This makes them structurally distinctive but they have equal status as full pairwise traits.

#### Four triple traits

Each triple trait is defined by which basic motivation is **absent**. The “absent basic motivation” is a geometric property of the trait, not a constitutive functional claim — the character of each triple trait comes from the three basic motivations that are active.

|Triple       |Components |Absent     |Low Pole          |High Pole|
|-------------|-----------|-----------|------------------|---------|
|**Neurotic** |B × M × Af |Autonomy   |Emotionally Stable|Neurotic |
|**Dominant** |B × Af × Au|Mastery    |Submissive        |Dominant |
|**Impulsive**|B × M × Au |Affiliation|Self-Controlled   |Impulsive|
|**Deceptive**|M × Af × Au|Boldness   |Honest/Humble     |Deceptive|

#### The derived trait

The derived trait at the ape and human levels is **Appetite for Action** (§01.12), with four faces (D, A, Z, F) corresponding to which basic motivation leads.

#### What the activation architecture delivers to personality psychology

The activation architecture predicts which personality constructs the field has independently discovered. The activation traits at the ape level are not labels invented by the project — they are, with two exceptions, names the field already uses for measured constructs.

- The four ring-adjacent pairwise traits are **four of the Big Five**: Conscientiousness, Agreeableness, Extraversion, Openness/Intellect.
- The fifth Big Five trait, **Neuroticism**, is the only triple trait that lacks Autonomy — sitting at the triple level rather than the basic-motivation level. This is the architectural reason Neuroticism keeps factor-analytically misbehaving as a higher-order construct rather than a clean basic motivation.
- The two diagonal pairwise traits are constructs the field already uses: **Core Self-Concept** (Judge’s Core Self-Evaluations literature; the sociometer tradition) at B×Af, and **Strategic Self-Concept** (Snyder’s Self-Monitoring literature) at Au×M.
- The three triple traits that include Autonomy are the **Dark Triad**: Dominant (narcissism), Impulsive (psychopathy), Deceptive (Machiavellianism). Schreiber & Marcus (2020) meta-analytically established these share a common higher-order factor D, orthogonal to the Stability and Plasticity meta-traits.
- The derived trait is **Appetite for Action**, the project’s new construct (§01.12).

The architecture is therefore a structural thesis about why personality psychology has converged on the constructs it has converged on — not a parallel theory using its own terminology.

### 01.11 Nested Activation Traits

The activation architecture is a hierarchy of nested traits: lower-order traits geometrically contain higher-order traits. A triple trait sits inside each of the three pairwise traits whose basic motivations it includes. The derived trait sits inside all six pairwise traits and all four triple traits.

**All nesting relationships are activation-only.** Nesting describes the geometry of which basic motivations are active in which composite trait, not the integration quality that binds them. Integration quality runs in parallel and can vary independently at any level (§01.15).

**Pairwise nesting at the ape/human level:**

|Pairwise trait                         |Contains triple traits|Plus    |
|---------------------------------------|----------------------|--------|
|Conscientious (B×M)                    |Neurotic, Impulsive   |Appetite|
|Agreeable (M×Af)                       |Neurotic, Deceptive   |Appetite|
|Extraverted (Af×Au)                    |Dominant, Deceptive   |Appetite|
|Open / Open-Intellect (Au×B)           |Dominant, Impulsive   |Appetite|
|Core Self-Concept (B×Af, diagonal)     |Neurotic, Dominant    |Appetite|
|Strategic Self-Concept (Au×M, diagonal)|Impulsive, Deceptive  |Appetite|

**Each triple trait is contained by three pairwise traits** — the three whose basic motivations it includes. The absent basic motivation identifies which pairwise does NOT contain it:

- Neurotic (no Autonomy) is NOT contained by Extraverted, Open, or Strategic Self-Concept.
- Dominant (no Mastery) is NOT contained by Conscientious, Agreeable, or Strategic Self-Concept.
- Impulsive (no Affiliation) is NOT contained by Agreeable, Extraverted, or Core Self-Concept.
- Deceptive (no Boldness) is NOT contained by Conscientious, Open, or Core Self-Concept.

**Appetite for Action is contained by every pairwise trait and every triple trait.** This is geometric — the derived trait is inside every lower-order trait by definition.

### 01.12 Appetite for Action

The derived trait at the ape and human levels is **Appetite for Action** (short: Appetite). It is an activation construct: it measures how intensely the organism runs gambling policies across all composite configurations of the four basic motivations simultaneously.

**Appetite is bipolar, not pathological at the high end.** Healthy individuals vary widely in Appetite. Low-Appetite profiles are quiet, steady, low-intensity across all configurations — not disordered. High-Appetite profiles are intense, engaged, expressive across all configurations — not disordered. Pathology appears at the tails, as it does for metabolic appetite: a healthy person can have a large appetite for food, for engagement, for work, for risk. Tail behavior (binge eating; obsessive work; relational enmeshment; reckless action) is where pathological extremes live, not across the healthy high range.

This bipolar framing is the principal reason “Worldly” was repositioned as the architectural label for the derived trait. Worldly had been treated as if high = coherent/successful and low = disordered, which imports a normative valence into a structural construct. Appetite has no such asymmetry. Worldliness is now an integration construct (§01.19).

#### The four faces of Appetite

Within a high-Appetite profile, one basic motivation typically leads. Four faces correspond to which basic motivation leads.

|Lead basic motivation|Face      |Short|Low pole             |High pole (healthy)                                           |Pathological high                            |
|------------|----------|-----|---------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
|Autonomy    |Dark Triad|**D**|meek, deferential    |independent, self-governing, willing to override consensus    |exploitation, callousness                    |
|Affiliation |Attached  |**A**|aloof, detached      |warm, bonded, relationally present, emotionally available     |enmeshment, relational drama, boundary loss  |
|Boldness    |Zeal      |**Z**|phlegmatic, low-drive|vital, approach-oriented, first to engage, willing to confront|burnout, recklessness, inability to disengage|
|Mastery     |Fervor    |**F**|uncommitted, hobbyist|dedicated, craft-focused, deep in competence-pursuit          |perfectionism, workaholism, craft-as-identity|

Each face is a profile of high activation across the three triple traits that contain the leading basic motivation, shaped by which basic motivation leads. The four faces are not types — they are profiles in a continuous space. A given individual has coordinates along all four dimensions; “Z-led” means Zeal is the largest component.

**Empirical anchor for D as a face.** Schreiber & Marcus (2020) meta-analytically established that the common variance across narcissism, Machiavellianism, and psychopathy constitutes a higher-order factor D, and that D is orthogonal to the Stability (Alpha) and Plasticity (Beta) meta-traits — that is, it is not reducible to the activation-vs-stability dimensions of the FFM hierarchy. This is what makes D a clean face of Appetite rather than a relabeling of low Agreeableness or low Conscientiousness. A, Z, and F are proposed by symmetry; their empirical validation is an open research program (§01.01 Tier 2).

#### What Appetite is not

- **Not the integration architecture.** Integration quality — how well the four activated systems bind into coherent states — runs orthogonally and has its own accretion structure (§01.15–§01.16).
- **Not pathological at the high end.** See bipolar framing above.
- **Not Worldliness.** Worldliness is an integration construct. See §01.19.
- **Not Domesticated.** Domesticated is the ape-level integration construct, not an activation construct. See §01.17.

### 01.13 Activation Emergence and Differentiation

The activation architecture grows through breakthroughs in evolution. Each new basic motivation produces a new ring on the Venn diagram and a new **derived trait** — the trait that integrates the new basic motivation with all earlier ones.

At each stack level, one trait occupies the derived-trait role: it measures all-basic-motivations-active-at-once at that level. When a new basic motivation arrives, the previous derived trait undergoes **differentiation** — its extension contracts to the composite that excludes the newly-arrived basic motivation. A new derived trait emerges. The earlier derived trait becomes an **ancestral trait** at the current stack level.

**Items are geometrically stationary; names narrow.** The facets that make up the derived trait at an earlier level are the same facets that make up the derived trait at later levels — they have not moved. What changes is that a trait’s name applies to a progressively more restricted subset of the activation architecture as new derived-trait names come online. The vertebrate-level Conscientious is the derived trait at that level; its extension covers broad generalized engagement. At endotherm level, Neurotic becomes the derived trait and its extension covers that same generalized engagement content; Conscientious retains only the B×M-specific content that does not involve Af. At ape level, Appetite becomes the derived trait; Neurotic retains only the B×M×Af content that does not involve Au. At each step, the ancestral-trait name applies to fewer items.

|Stack Level   |Basic motivations present|Derived trait                      |Differentiated ancestral traits                                  |
|--------------|-------------------------|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
|0.2 Bilaterian|B                        |(none — only one basic motivation) |—                                                                |
|0.3 Vertebrate|B, M                     |**Conscientious** (B×M)            |—                                                                |
|1 Endotherm   |B, M, Af                 |**Neurotic** (B×M×Af)              |Conscientious                                                    |
|2 Ape         |B, M, Af, Au             |**Appetite for Action** (B×M×Af×Au)|Conscientious, Neurotic, and all other pairwise and triple traits|
|3 Human       |B, M, Af, Au (same four) |**Appetite for Action** (same)     |No new basic motivation; activation structure unchanged          |

**Vertebrate → Endotherm transition.** Affiliation arrives. Conscientious, which had been the derived trait, undergoes differentiation: it now refers only to B×M content excluding Af. Neurotic (B×M×Af) emerges as the new derived trait.

**Endotherm → Ape transition.** Autonomy arrives. Neurotic, which had been the derived trait, undergoes differentiation: it now refers only to B×M×Af content excluding Au. Appetite for Action (B×M×Af×Au) emerges as the new derived trait.

**Ape → Human transition.** No new basic motivation arrives. The activation structure is identical at ape and human levels. The only refinement: the Open pairwise trait (Au×B) is usually labeled **Open/Intellect** at the human level, reflecting the additional symbolic-compression content that human culture puts into that trait. Geometry unchanged.

**The Dominant transformation.** Dominant exists as a *pairwise trait* (B×Af) at the endotherm level — approach tendency in a bonded social context. When Autonomy arrives at the ape level, B×Af persists as the pairwise trait Core Self-Concept. Dominant transitions from being the endotherm-level triple (B×M×Af, all three endotherm basic motivations) to being a different triple (B×Af×Au, absent M). Driven by what Autonomy adds, not by what it removes. The “breaks the adjacency” narrative in some legacy docs is superseded.

**Vertical and lateral differentiation.** The mechanism described here operates vertically across stack levels: at each breakthrough in evolution, a new basic motivation arrives and differentiates the previous derived trait. The Pan Personality Puzzle (§01.14) describes the same mechanism running laterally within a single stack level: ecological pressure on one basic-motivation boundary differentiates a specific triple trait from the derived trait. Both are differentiation events; they differ only in what drives them (a breakthrough in evolution vs. ecological selection) and in their scale.

**This is a prediction.** If the differentiation pattern holds, then at each stack level with a new basic motivation, there should be a measurable dimension corresponding to the derived trait that behaves as a joint-activation construct — not explainable by lower-order traits. Testable in comparative animal studies: vertebrate general-Conscientiousness (broadly defined, including items that load on Neuroticism and Appetite in humans), endotherm general-Neuroticism (similarly), ape-level Appetite. See §01.01 (Tier 1).

**Measurement implication for comparative psychology.** Modern human Conscientiousness scales have been progressively differentiated across three breakthroughs in evolution. Items that would have loaded on Conscientiousness at the vertebrate level now load on Neuroticism or Appetite in humans. Porting modern human Conscientiousness instruments to non-primate vertebrates will systematically undercount the trait’s variance because the instrument has been stripped of its most general cross-system items. The “correct” vertebrate-level Conscientiousness scale should include items that in humans load on Neuroticism and Appetite.

### 01.14 The Pan Personality Puzzle

The same 54-item Hominoid Personality Questionnaire administered across chimpanzee and bonobo populations produces six factors every time — but never the same six. Which factor coheres and which dissolves tracks the ecology of the population, not just sample size.

**The puzzle.** Dominance is the most stable chimp factor; in bonobos it is relabeled Assertiveness with shifted composition, and in wild bonobos no clear Dominance factor emerged at all. Neuroticism emerges in zoo chimps but not lab chimps and not bonobos. Attentiveness emerges only in bonobos. Conscientiousness, Openness, and Agreeableness are stable across both species.

**The resolution.** The activation architecture is fixed. What varies across populations is **which basic motivation’s boundary is sharpened by ecological pressure**, and this differentiates the complementary triple trait — the one *missing* that basic motivation — from the derived trait. This is the same differentiation mechanism that operates vertically in §01.13, here running laterally within a single stack level (driven by ecological selection) rather than vertically across levels (driven by a breakthrough in evolution).

The counterintuitive prediction: high Mastery pressure does not sharpen Conscientiousness. It sharpens **Dominant** (the triple trait missing Mastery), because Mastery pulling clean away from the derived trait gives the remaining three-motivation blend its own identity.

|Ecology                                           |Basic motivation sharpened|Triple trait that differentiates       |Pan evidence                                                       |
|--------------------------------------------------|-----------------|---------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Coalitionary competition, hierarchical enforcement|Mastery          |Dominant (B×Af×Au, absent M)           |Dominance is the most robust chimp factor                          |
|Prosocial coordination, egalitarian structure     |Affiliation      |Impulsive/Attentive (B×M×Au, absent Af)|Attentiveness is the cleanest species-differentiating bonobo factor|
|Autonomy salient (often frustrated)               |Autonomy         |Neurotic (B×M×Af, absent Au)           |Neuroticism emerges in zoo chimps, not lab chimps or bonobos       |
|High-risk signaling, moral authority              |Boldness         |Deceptive (M×Af×Au, absent B)          |No clean Pan Deceptive factor identified yet (most speculative)    |

The puzzle’s resolution is the project’s most direct empirical demonstration that the activation architecture is doing real work — the activation traits are not arbitrary labels but population-differentiable structural locations. Full evidence in Notes on Personality.

# Part IV — Integration

This Part specifies the integration reading of the architecture: what integration is, how it accretes, the constructs that measure it at different Stack levels, the inheritance gradient that organizes its content across levels, the special status of Worldliness as the human-level interface, and the two-coordinate disorder mapping that integration enables.

### 01.15 The Integration Architecture

Integration is not organized as a set of activation traits. It is a Bennett-style accretion of supervisory interfaces between activation cascades. Each integration interface is the relationship by which a later Bennett layer modulates an earlier one.

|Interface  |What it binds                            |Mechanism                                                                                                                                      |Weak-link pathology                                         |
|-----------|-----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
|V↔B        |Vertebrate RL ↔ bilaterian valence       |Basal ganglia learning over reflexive approach/avoid                                                                                           |Dysregulated approach/avoid, panic, phobia                  |
|M↔(earlier)|Endotherm simulation ↔ earlier layers    |aPFC 1st-order modeling, limbic bonding machinery                                                                                              |Attachment disorganization, bonding pathology               |
|A↔(earlier)|Ape mentalizing ↔ earlier layers         |gPFC/STS/TPJ 2nd-order models                                                                                                                  |Impaired self-directed agency, theory-of-mind failures      |
|H↔(earlier)|Human symbolic machinery ↔ earlier layers|Deacon-symbolic reference (arbitrary conventional signs organized in relational systems); meme-to-gene ratio as developmental exposure variable|Parochialism, poor cultural integration, fragmented identity|

**Integration is operationalized as quality of supervisory relationships, not intensity or activation trait.**

**Bennett-nested supervision over disjoint scopes.** Integration layers accrete in strict phylogenetic order, and each layer’s supervisory direction points backward — later tries to explain earlier, following Bennett’s kludge architecture. But each layer’s *scope* (what activation traits it binds) is disjoint from earlier layers’ scope. Two orthogonal integration dimensions run simultaneously at the human level:

- **Stability-scope integration** — binds Au-absent activation traits (Conscientious, Agreeable, Core Self-Concept, Neurotic). Seeded at the endotherm level.
- **Plasticity-scope integration** — binds Au-containing activation traits (Extraverted, Open, Strategic Self-Concept, Dominant, Impulsive, Deceptive, Appetite). Seeded at the ape level.

These scopes do not contain each other. DeYoung’s empirical finding that the Stability and Plasticity meta-traits are essentially uncorrelated is the architectural prediction of disjoint scope. Bennett-nested supervision remains: Plasticity-scope integration supervises Stability-scope integration content (mentalizing models attachment / RL / reflex activity), but binds its own distinct set of activation traits.

**Diagnostic logic: weakest supervisory link within scope.** Pathology localizes by scope, not by position in a nested hierarchy. A weak link in Stability-scope produces disorganization localized to B/M/Af-containing activity (internalizing-flavored presentations). A weak link in Plasticity-scope produces disorganization localized to Au-containing activity (mentalizing and self-monitoring failures, some externalizing presentations). The level of the weak link predicts the shape of the pathology.

This is the architectural source of dissociations like successful vs. unsuccessful psychopathy: same activation profile (Dark Triad / D-led Appetite) at different integration coordinates produces different clinical presentations. Successful psychopathy = strong Plasticity-scope integration (A↔earlier intact) despite the antisocial activation profile; unsuccessful psychopathy = weak Plasticity-scope integration on the same activation profile.

### 01.16 Integration Accretion

Integration machinery accretes as activation traits emerge. Each Stack level that has multiple systems contributes integration content, and two disjoint integration dimensions are now visible at the ape and human levels.

|Stack Level   |Stability-scope integration                                                          |Plasticity-scope integration                                                                |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|0.3 Vertebrate|Seeded (unnamed; upstream substrate, not a personality-level construct in humans)    |—                                                                                           |
|1 Endotherm   |Stability meta-trait / Common EF (predicted same construct at different descriptions)|—                                                                                           |
|2 Ape         |Carries forward                                                                      |**Domesticated**                                                                            |
|3 Human       |Carries forward (Stability meta-trait / Common EF)                                   |**Plasticity meta-trait / Worldliness** (predicted same construct at different descriptions)|

**Two-dimension structure.** Stability and Plasticity meta-traits are architecturally orthogonal (§01.15: disjoint scope). That DeYoung finds them essentially uncorrelated empirically is the architectural prediction of disjoint scope, not an anomaly to be explained.

**Domesticated and Worldliness as same-coin channels.** Both are Plasticity-scope integration measured at different Stack levels: Domesticated at the ape level, Worldliness at the human level. The self-domestication phenotype (tolerance, novelty engagement, extended social repertoire under human captivity — the behaviors that *open up* in domesticated apes, not only the suppressed-reactive-aggression aspect) is Plasticity-flavored. Worldliness — high meme-to-gene ratio, cosmopolitanism — is the human-level expression of the same integration dimension.

This reclassification resolves a prior ambiguity: the genealogical claim that Domesticated “persists as the substrate under Worldliness” now reads cleanly — both are Plasticity-scope integration channels at different phylogenetic scopes.

### 01.17 Integration Constructs

The integration architecture is measured by several constructs operating at different Stack levels and different levels of description (personality-trait, cognitive-mechanism, neural-substrate). The project predicts these constructs converge within each of two integration dimensions (Stability-scope and Plasticity-scope).

**Stability meta-trait.** Higher-order personality factor: common variance underlying Agreeableness, Conscientiousness, and (low) Neuroticism. Lewis (2016): Stability predicted uncinate fasciculus FA (β = 0.27), and after partialling Stability, the residuals for A, C, and N showed zero independent association with uncinate FA. Empirical anchor for Stability-scope integration at the personality-trait level.

**Common EF.** Shared variance across executive function tasks (inhibition, updating, shifting). Friedman & Miyake’s literature. Hatoum (2023): GABAergic substrate, transdiagnostic predictive power after IQ control. Candidate cognitive-mechanism measure of Stability-scope integration.

**Plasticity meta-trait.** Higher-order personality factor: common variance underlying Extraversion and Openness/Intellect. Architecturally orthogonal to Stability (disjoint scope; §01.15). Empirical anchor for Plasticity-scope integration at the personality-trait level.

*Integration-face caveat.* DeYoung’s original Plasticity framing admits both an integration-flavored reading (coherent binding of Autonomy-containing systems under symbolic supervision) and an activation-flavored reading (dopaminergic exploratory variability, approach to novelty). The project uses the integration face. Activation-flavored exploratory-variability is covered elsewhere in the architecture (Boldness-led Appetite, the Z face); it is not what “Plasticity” here refers to.

**Domesticated.** Ape-level channel of Plasticity-scope integration. Self-domestication phenotype at the behavioral-developmental level — specifically the repertoire that opens up under captivity (tolerance, novelty engagement, extended social repertoire), which is Plasticity-flavored rather than Stability-flavored.

**Worldliness.** Human-level channel of Plasticity-scope integration. High meme-to-gene ratio produces cosmopolitanism — the symbolic-cultural calibration that binds the individual’s Autonomy-containing systems to the cohort’s symbolic package. See §01.19.

**p-factor.** General psychopathology factor. Empirical support is mixed: neural substrates (pontine white matter, corpus callosum body) are consistent with an integration-failure reading, but psychometric evidence for p as unique variance beyond specific spectra is weak (Watts 2019, Forbes 2023).

#### Convergence predictions

The project predicts two convergences, one per integration dimension:

**Stability-scope convergence.** Stability meta-trait and Common EF are the same underlying integration construct measured at different levels of description (personality-trait vs. cognitive-mechanism). Predicts strong convergence when both are properly modeled in the same sample after partialling out activation-level variance. The deep research reconnaissance flagged this test as the single highest-value empirical gap in the activation-versus-integration literature. **The test has not been performed.**

**Plasticity-scope convergence.** Plasticity meta-trait, Domesticated, and Worldliness are the same underlying integration construct measured at personality-trait, behavioral-developmental (captive-ape repertoire), and cultural-calibration levels of description. Predicts convergence across these three channels after partialling out activation-level variance and Stability-scope integration variance.

If either prediction holds, the integration architecture has a clean multi-level structure within that dimension. If either fails, that dimension may be multi-dimensional rather than a single dimension with multiple measurements. Either outcome is informative.

### 01.18 The Inheritance Gradient

How regulatory content gets transmitted across the supervisory hierarchy. As the Stack accretes, new transmission channels open and the dominant inheritance channel shifts upward.

|Level         |Dominant Inheritance Channel                                              |
|--------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|System 0      |Genetic                                                                   |
|System 0.1–0.3|Genetic + parental effects                                                |
|System 1      |+ epigenetic + social learning (indexical semiotics)                      |
|System 2      |+ strategic social transmission (mentalizing enables teaching with intent)|
|System 3      |+ symbolic cultural transmission (becomes dominant)                       |

**Danchin’s (2011) inclusive heritability** unifies all channels: total parent-offspring transmission regardless of mechanism, summing across genetic, epigenetic, parental, social, and cultural inputs. **Turkheimer’s (2014) phenotypic null** provides cover at the genetic level: massive polygenicity with tiny per-variant effects means the developmental environment does real causal work.

**Why this is integration content.** The inheritance gradient describes how the supervisory cascades acquire their content across generations. The integration architecture (§01.15) is *what binds* activation layers together; the inheritance gradient is *how the binding gets its content*. Each Stack level’s integration mechanism uses the inheritance channels that level adds. Symbolic cultural transmission at System 3 is what makes the H↔(earlier) interface (Worldliness) possible at all — without symbolic compression, you cannot transmit “we overdid X last generation, compensate with Y” across cohort boundaries.

### 01.19 Worldliness and Meme-to-Gene Ratio

**Worldliness** is a candidate construct at the H↔(earlier) integration interface. **Definition:** the extent to which an individual’s regulatory architecture is shaped by their generation’s symbolic package versus by the genetic, epigenetic, and within-family inheritance channels of the lower Stack levels. Operationalized as profile similarity (ICC or cosine) between an individual’s personality and their generation’s centroid.

**Architectural position.** Worldliness sits on the integration architecture, NOT at the derived trait. The prior framing — Worldliness as the derived trait of the activation architecture — was an error and has been retired. The derived trait is Appetite for Action (§01.12). Worldliness is the human-level channel of Plasticity-scope integration, alongside Domesticated (the ape-level channel of the same dimension) and the Plasticity meta-trait (the personality-trait-level channel). See §01.16–01.17.

**Mechanism: the Meme-to-Gene Ratio.**

Definition: (time in meme-carrying activities) / (time in gene-carrying activities) during the formative window (~ages 10–22).

- **Numerator (meme):** Unstructured peer time, solo media consumption, social media use.
- **Denominator (gene):** Solitary non-media time, family “just us” time.

The ratio is a developmental exposure variable. It indexes how much of the formative window is spent acquiring symbolic-cultural content from the cohort versus genetic-and-parental content from the family. High meme-to-gene ratio predicts high Worldliness.

**Market basket approach (v1.0).** Uses only unambiguous items. Ambiguous activities (sibling interaction, family media co-viewing, structured institutional time, religious practice, reading, team sports) excluded pending empirical clarification. Full discussion in Notes on Personality.

**Why this is integration content.** Worldliness measures the quality and origin of the supervisory binding at the H↔(earlier) interface. It is NOT a measure of how intensely the four basic motivations activate. It is a measure of where the regulatory content controlling that activation comes from — symbolic culture (high) or gene-and-family (low). The mechanism (meme-to-gene ratio) is load-bearing and committed; “Worldliness” as the name for the measurement construct is retained because the construct belongs on the Plasticity-scope integration dimension (§01.16, §01.17).

### 01.20 Disorder Mapping

A disorder gets two coordinates:

- **Activation coordinate:** which activation traits are dominant (which basic motivations, which pairwise traits, which triple traits, with what intensity).
- **Integration coordinate:** which supervisory interface in the cascade is weakest.

**Same activation + different weak link = different disorder.** This is the architectural source of the successful/unsuccessful psychopathy dissociation: same Dark Triad / D-led Appetite activation, different weak link in the integration stack. Successful psychopaths have intact A↔(earlier) integration despite their activation profile; unsuccessful psychopaths have weak A↔(earlier) integration on the same profile. The activation coordinate predicts the personality style; the integration coordinate predicts whether the style produces functional or disordered behavior.

**Different activation + same weak link = disorders sharing a common integration failure across activation phenotypes.** This is the architectural source of the transdiagnostic Common EF / Stability findings: weak M↔(earlier) integration produces a recognizable signature across multiple activation profiles.

**Principles.**

1. A disorder can only exist at a phylogenetic level if the architectural trait it occupies is available at that level.
2. No basic-motivation extreme is a true disorder. Disorders live at pairwise traits, triple traits, the derived trait, and integration interfaces.
3. The internalizing/externalizing divide IS the Autonomy divide. Internalizing (Neurotic triple trait) = the only triple trait lacking Autonomy. All three externalizing spectra live on Autonomy-bearing triple traits.

**Empirical correspondences (Tier 2):**

- **Common EF / Stability meta-trait** index Stability-scope integration — primarily V↔B and M↔(earlier) interfaces. Weak-link signature: internalizing-flavored presentations.
- **Mentalizing quality** indexes the A↔(earlier) interface within Plasticity-scope integration.
- **Plasticity meta-trait / Domesticated / Worldliness** index Plasticity-scope integration — H↔(earlier) and A↔(earlier) supervisory binding of Autonomy-containing systems. Weak-link signature: mentalizing and self-monitoring failures; symbolic-cultural miscalibration.
- **p-factor** indexes endotherm- and primate-level integration failure. Predicts: p-factor should correlate with Common EF and mentalizing measures but *not* with Worldliness. Testable in ABCD and Generation R.

Full disorder-by-trait mapping table is in Notes on Personality. The two-coordinate framing supersedes the prior “p-factor = extreme low Worldly center” formulation.

# Part V — Civilization Level

This Part extends the architecture to the civilization level. The extension is structurally analogous to the organism level — same logic of activation pools, supervisory integration, weakest-link pathology — but it runs on coupled-many-individuals as substrate, unfolds in historical rather than evolutionary time, and is much smaller as a portion of the project. The load-bearing constraint: this Part should occupy roughly the theoretical real estate of the pre-bilaterian section of the organism Stack. We are NOT building a generalized theory of stacks-of-stacks.

Most claims in this Part are Tier 2 to Tier 3 (§01.01).

### 01.21 Civilization-Level Activation

Civilization-level activation pools are classes of **superallostatic gambles** — collective predictive resource mobilization by coordinated-many-individuals. Structurally analogous to organism-level allostatic gambles, but running on the substrate of many coupled humans rather than one nervous system.

|Pool             |Class of collective gamble                                             |Overreach                                                                  |Underreach                                                    |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Hard power**   |Coercive capacity, territorial security                                |Imperial overextension, military spending at expense of internal investment|Loss of capacity to resist external pressure                  |
|**Macroeconomic**|Productive capacity, resource flow                                     |Credit bubbles, over-investment in declining sectors                       |Infrastructure decay, de-industrialization without replacement|
|**Soft power**   |Cultural/ideological/normative reach                                   |Ideological overreach, cultural imperialism triggering backlash            |Civilizational introversion, memetic stagnation               |
|**Sovereignty**  |Capacity for independent strategic commitment as a decision-making unit|Autarky, ideological self-sealing                                          |Loss of independent identity, dissolution/capture             |

**Sovereignty as civ-level analog of Autonomy.** Both name the unit’s capacity to commit to strategies that override external or peer consensus. Orthogonality is empirically supported: high-Sovereignty / low-other (Tokugawa Japan, North Korea); low-Sovereignty / high-other (vassal states, some post-colonial configurations). “Sovereignty” here names the broader capacity-for-independent-strategic-commitment; Westphalian sovereignty is one historical instantiation. Ancient independent poleis and premodern empires exercised the capacity in different forms.

**No confirmed derived trait at the civ level.** The organism-level parallel (Appetite for Action as the derived trait integrating all four basic motivations) may or may not have a civilizational analog. Demographics was previously offered as that derived trait, but the more accurate architectural position is that demographics is the **foundational metabolic substrate** on which all four pools run — analogous to organism-level allostasis (§01.02), which is also not an activation system. Coupled-many-individuals is what the pools gamble with and what the cascades bind. Demographic collapse therefore hollows capacity to run any of the pools, not because demographics is a low-activation derived trait but because it is substrate failure.

Whether there is a separable civ-level total-superallostatic-commitment construct that parallels Appetite for Action is open. Civilizations may genuinely lack a derived trait of this kind — the four pools may run substantially independently.

#### Historical emergence

Civilization-level activation pools emerge in **historical** time. Each pool was seeded at a specific historical transition.

|Pool         |Approximate historical seeding                       |Transition                                                                                                                                        |
|-------------|-----------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Hard power   |Pre-state, intensified at state formation (~3000 BCE)|Coercive coordination pre-dates recorded history; state-level elaboration from Sumer onward                                                       |
|Macroeconomic|State formation (~3000 BCE)                          |Sumerian temple-complex economies, Pharaonic storage, taxation                                                                                    |
|Soft power   |Axial Age (~800–200 BCE)                             |Universalizing ideological systems claiming validity outside civilization of origin: Greek philosophy, Buddhism, Confucianism, Abrahamic religions|
|Sovereignty  |Early modern (~1648 CE)                              |Developed state system with mutual recognition of independence. Westphalian system canonical; older Greco-Roman and Chinese imperial roots        |

**The civilization-level Stack is still accreting.** The current historical moment is mid-accretion, not end-of-Stack.

**Historical seeding timeline is pending historiographical review (§02.14.3).** The four-pool list and Sovereignty-as-fourth-pool claim are resolved (§02.14.1); specific historical placements remain open pending review.

### 01.22 Civilization-Level Integration

Three integration cascades, corresponding to substrates operating at progressively longer timescales. From Notes on Civilization.

|Cascade|Substrate    |Timescale                                 |Reference type (Deacon)|
|-------|-------------|------------------------------------------|-----------------------|
|1      |Experiential |~40–80 yr (communicative memory, Assmann) |Indexical              |
|2      |Institutional|~200–300 yr (secular cycle, Turchin)      |Symbolic               |
|3      |Deep symbolic|~1000 yr (asabiyya cycle, Khaldun/Turchin)|Constitutive symbolic  |

**Inter-civilizational selection is not a cascade — it is the selection environment.** Cascades are internal supervisory layers (§01.15). Inter-civ selection is environmental pressure, categorically the same kind of thing as natural selection acting on organisms. Natural selection is not one of the organism’s integration cascades; it is the environment that shapes them over evolutionary time. By parallel, inter-civ selection is the selection environment in which the three civ-level cascades are shaped and pruned.

**Input from below.** The first input to the civilization-level integration stack is individual-level Worldliness / meme-to-gene ratio / superallostasis. Worldliness feeds directly into the civilization-level experiential cascade: it is the individual-level interface that binds organism-level cognition to civilization-level symbolic machinery.

**Asabiyya ≈ Worldliness at scale.** Both are second-order properties measuring how much coordination, not what kind. Both generated by contact with external pressure forcing internal consolidation. Both decay when pressure dissipates. The parallel is structural, not metaphorical.

**Civilization-level pathology and the current historical moment.** Same two-coordinate logic as organism level: activation coordinate (which pool dominant, which activation traits engaged) + integration coordinate (which substrate weakest). Three failure modes identified in the standalone civilization docs:

- **Institutional antigenic sin** — institutional substrate maintaining the wrong-generation correction signal.
- **Experiential memory collapse** — accelerated dissipation of communicative memory.
- **Substrate inversion** — digital preservation making forgetting effortful and remembering default. Novel failure mode, no historical precedent.

The globalization thesis (Notes on Civilization): progressive dissolution of meta-ethnic frontiers degrades the *selection environment* — the between-civilization selection pressure that shaped and maintained each civilization’s internal coordination. Paired with substrate inversion, this produces a system that has simultaneously lost external selection pressure and internal correction mechanism. Tier 3.

### 01.23 The Generational Cycle

Four-phase rotation around the activation architecture’s ring topology, running on the civilization-level experiential cascade (§01.22, Cascade 1). The cycle is an activation phenomenon driven by an integration substrate.

1. **Civic Heroes** (high Affiliation, low Autonomy) → raise…
2. **Adaptive Artists** (high Autonomy, low Boldness) → raise…
3. **Idealistic Prophets** (high Boldness, low Mastery) → raise…
4. **Reactive Nomads** (high Mastery, low Affiliation) → raise Civic Heroes.

**Mechanism (abstract).** Allostatic predictive processing applied to the superorganism. Trade-offs between four ring vertices produce continuous error correction. Each generation experiences the previous generation’s excesses, parents in the opposite direction, produces children calibrated to compensate.

**Mechanism (concrete).** Symbolic compression makes the parenting correction signal transmissible across generational boundaries. Only a symbolic species can transmit “we overdid X, compensate with Y” across generations. The transmission runs on the experiential cascade — communicative memory across two-to-three generations.

**Why correction goes to the adjacent vertex, not the opposite.** Strauss-Howe observes a 4-cycle; opposite-correction would produce 2-cycles (two archetypes alternating); adjacent-correction is forced if the 4-cycle holds. Conditional on the 4-cycle being empirically real.

**Rotation direction.** Clockwise in phylogenetic emergence order: B (bilaterian) → M (vertebrate) → Af (endotherm) → Au (ape). The cycle runs Af → Au → B → M → Af, which is clockwise traversal in ring-order. The mechanism is Strauss-Howe’s parent-child compensation across turnings; the project’s contribution is noting that S-H’s cycle direction coincides with ring-order emergence — either a deep structural constraint or a shared feature of layered compensatory systems (open).

**Commitment on data.** The architecture commits to superposition of three components: secular trends (driven by inheritance-gradient shifts toward symbolic cultural transmission, §01.18), a long cycle (~80yr, S-H rotation), and short cycles (sub-turning compensatory adjustments). **Tier 3.** The generational cycle is one of the project’s most ambitious and most exposed predictions. Its failure would not impeach Tier 1 or Tier 2 architecture (§01.01 Firewall).

### §02.13 — Activation/Integration Mapping Pairings (Validation)

### §02.14 — Civilization-Level Architecture (Validation)

**Status: SPLIT** — four sub-items RESOLVED, one remains OPEN (April 17, 2026)

1. **The four-pool list including Sovereignty.** RESOLVED → CURRENT. Sovereignty is the civ-level analog of Autonomy: the unit’s capacity to commit to strategies overriding external or peer consensus. Orthogonality empirically supported by high-Sovereignty-low-other cases (Tokugawa Japan, North Korea) and low-Sovereignty-high-other cases (vassal states). 2. **Demographics as the derived trait.** RESOLVED → SUPERSEDED. Whether civilizations have any separable derived trait analogous to Appetite for Action is open; civ-level activation may genuinely lack such a construct. See §01.21.
3. **Historical pool-emergence timeline.** **OPEN — DEFERRED to historiographical review session.** Specific historical placements (especially Sovereignty → Westphalian) need scrutiny against actual historiography. The four-pool list and Sovereignty-as-fourth-pool claim are resolved in (1) above; this remaining item is about *when* each pool was seeded, not *whether* they belong.
4. **Inter-civilizational selection as fourth integration cascade.** RESOLVED → SUPERSEDED. Inter-civ selection is the **selection environment**, not a cascade. Cascades are internal supervisory layers; selection is environmental pressure. Parallel to natural selection acting on organisms — which is not one of the organism’s integration cascades. Civ-level integration has three cascades, not four. Globalization thesis sharpens accordingly: degradation of meta-ethnic frontiers degrades the selection environment that shaped the three cascades. Operationalized as **Deacon-symbolic reference**: arbitrary conventional signs organized in systems where cross-sign relations carry meaning. The architectural threshold is capacity to operate in such systems, not specific features (compositionality, displacement, recursion) which are features of symbolic systems rather than the threshold. Functional load: transmissibility of abstract compensatory corrections across generations (required by §01.23 generational cycle — can’t run on iconic or indexical reference). Species-threshold empirics remain contested; the architectural commitment doesn’t depend on those debates. See §01.15.

**Superseded formulation:** treating all five sub-items as VALIDATION-pending in one block.

### §02.15 — Kenrick Life Tasks vs. Activation Traits: Mapping Problem

**Status: RESOLVED → CURRENT** (April 17, 2026)

Working hypothesis from §01.05 holds: life tasks are pre-architecture selection pressures; basic motivations are evolved solutions; activation traits are the combinatorial geometry of those solutions. No 1:1 expected between problems and solutions. The 7-task-vs-7-trait count match at the endotherm level is arithmetic coincidence, not structural correspondence.

**Many-to-many deployment holds both ways.** Tasks recruit multiple basic motivations (Self-Protection draws on B, M, Af, Au; Status recruits all four; Parenting recruits Af, M, B, Au). Basic motivations serve multiple tasks (B and M serve all seven; Af serves five or six).

**Origin vs. deployment distinction added to §01.05.** Basic motivations have task-specific evolutionary origins — Affiliation’s oxytocin/vasopressin machinery arrived at endothermy under intensified Parenting and Mate Retention pressure — but their deployment is many-to-many. Task-to-basic-motivation origin is narrower than task-to-basic-motivation deployment.

**Empirical prediction.** Task-specific personality instruments (parenting-style measures, romantic-attachment measures) should not cleanly isolate single activation traits. The factor-analytic messiness of domain-specific personality instruments is consistent with this.

**Superseded formulation:** “Lines of Descent” row-by-row mapping table in prior §01.10 (already dropped in the Constitution; this resolves the §02 register entry).
