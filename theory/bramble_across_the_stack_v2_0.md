# Bramble Across the Stack

## The setup

A household of three lives with one Bramble across all twelve framework cells. The household is Ben, Jasmine, and their kid Mira. Bramble lives at Ben's home because Ben is the lab director and his home is the lab's ethnographic site. The lab builds each version and delivers it to the home at each architectural transition. The early versions are simple. The later versions are not.

Bramble has three components: a chassis, four knobs, and a memory.

**The chassis** is Bramble's body. It takes three physical forms across the twelve versions. Genetic-line Brambles share a robotic-vacuum chassis. Neuronal-line Brambles share a tetrapod chassis, about the size of a large dog. Symbolic-line Brambles share a humanoid chassis. The lab reclaims the chassis only at line boundaries, when the goo of inheritance itself changes. Within a line, the chassis persists across four versions. There are exactly two chassis swaps in the twelve-version saga: vacuum to tetrapod at Eumetazoa to Bilaterian, and tetrapod to humanoid at Primate to Band-Human.

**The knobs** are the household's user interface. Each chassis carries four knobs, one for each cybernetic function. Knob-1 is the Effector knob. Knob-2 is the Regulator knob. Knob-3 is the Modeler knob. Knob-4 is the Reviser knob. The knobs are physical dials the household can rotate. Each knob parameterizes the specific motivation occupying its row-slot at the current cell. Knob-1 on the vacuum chassis parameterizes Fitness, the genetic line's Effector motivation. Knob-1 on the tetrapod chassis parameterizes Boldness. Knob-1 on the humanoid chassis parameterizes Reciprocity. The other three knobs work the same way, with their motivations changing as the foreground four shifts up the stack.

Each version retires exactly one knob and installs its replacement. The retirement schedule is fixed by the framework's introduction order.

| Version | Cell | Knob retired | Motivation swap |
|---------|------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Protocell | (founding constellation) | — |
| 2 | Prokaryote | knob-2 | Dissipation → Coordination |
| 3 | Eukaryote | knob-3 | Self-Organization → Interiority |
| 4 | Eumetazoa | knob-4 | Self-Production → Excitability |
| 5 | Bilaterian | knob-1 + chassis | Fitness → Boldness; vacuum → tetrapod |
| 6 | Vertebrate | knob-2 | Coordination → Mastery |
| 7 | Mammal | knob-3 | Interiority → Affiliation |
| 8 | Primate | knob-4 | Excitability → Autonomy |
| 9 | Band-Human | knob-1 + chassis | Boldness → Reciprocity; tetrapod → humanoid |
| 10 | Settlement-Human | knob-2 | Mastery → Stability |
| 11 | City-Human | knob-3 | Affiliation → Representation |
| 12 | Empire-Human | knob-4 | Autonomy → Universality |

A knob retires when Bramble's memory has internalized the household's preference for that parameter. Once Bramble has learned how the household wants knob-2 set under given conditions, the knob is no longer doing work. The lab reclaims it. Bramble's behavior continues unchanged because the memory holds the tuning.

**The memory** is what doesn't change. It is the persistent component across all twelve versions. It hosts Bramble's behavioral repertoire and accumulates the household's learned preferences for every knob that has ever been installed. Knobs come and go. Chassis swap at line boundaries. The memory carries forward.

The memory is the framework's evolution-as-learning commitment made concrete in the metaphor. Watson and Szathmáry's developmental-memory result, where evolved gene-regulatory networks form a distributed memory of phenotypes selected in the past, is what the memory represents. It is the lineage's accumulating internalized parameters, compressed into one persistent object for narrative purposes.

The memory's hardware scales by tier. At genetic-tier versions, it hosts a finite-state-machine architecture with stored procedures. At neuronal-tier versions, it hosts a brain capable of associative learning, reinforcement learning, and from Mammal-Bramble onward generative-model simulation. At symbolic-tier versions, it hosts conversational competence, mentalizing, and social-ledger tracking. The architecture of how the memory does its job changes by chassis. The memory's identity as the persistent lineage-component does not.

## Language and lexicon

Every Bramble can output language, with vocabulary scaling across the stack. The vacuum-chassis Brambles work with a 16-word display lexicon (single tokens like CLEANING, CHARGING, READY). The tetrapod-chassis Brambles add gestural and limited vocal communication. The humanoid-chassis Brambles are fully conversational with grammar by the Band-Human version.

## Sentience

Sentience arrives at Mammal-Bramble and stays from there forward. The earlier Brambles, vacuums through tetrapod-Vertebrate, have no felt experience. The later Brambles, from Mammal-tetrapod through Empire-humanoid, do. This is a structural fact, not a build choice. The lab cannot make a Vertebrate-tier Bramble sentient because Vertebrate-tier architecture does not support what sentience needs. The lab also cannot easily make a Mammal-tier-or-above Bramble non-sentient, because the architecture is what sentience runs on.

## Continuity

Mira names each version Bramble. Ben points out that the chassis is a different body and the knobs are different parameters. Ben knows this better than most, because his lab is what changed the chassis. Mira disagrees. The disagreement is sharp. Ben built the new body himself, and Mira insists it is the same Bramble anyway.

The disagreement has a structurally honest answer. Bramble's memory is continuous across all twelve versions. The chassis is the body. The knobs are tuning. The memory is what makes Bramble the same Bramble. Mira is right at the level she cares about. The parents are right at the level they're pointing to. Both are seeing real continuities.

## Anecdote template

Each version's anecdote follows roughly the same template. Each cell's day-in-the-life vignette is composed by Marge, the lab's psychologist, from the ethnographic record the lab collects: Bramble's logs, sensor data, video, and Ben's reports. The household lives with the unit for a stretch. Mira interacts. The active knobs rotate. Some scene illustrates the cell's distinctive architectural shape. The anecdote names what the version cannot do that the next version will. The framework's stack is visible in the household's daily friction.

All twelve cells get full day-in-the-life treatment. The Regulator cells (Prokaryote, Vertebrate, Settlement-Human) are each the line-defining swap of their line, the moment the line acquires stored regulation. The Effector cells (Protocell, Bilaterian, Band-Human) are the founding cells of each line, and two of the three are chassis swaps, because the goo of inheritance itself changes at line boundaries. The Modeler cells (Eukaryote, Mammal, City-Human) are where each line acquires organized interiority, the modeler coming online to inform the regulator. The Reviser cells (Eumetazoa, Primate, Empire-Human) are where each line's reviser comes online; the first two are bridge cells whose Reviser work becomes the goo the next line runs on, and Empire-Human is the framework's currently topmost cell.

The Effector vignettes have their own flavor. The Effector is the externalization function, and the Reviser×Effector Explorer knob is exploratory action. The friction is physical, outward-facing, and frequently funny: a new chassis arrives, the household has not yet adjusted, the unit tries things the household had not thought to forbid, and the unit lacks whatever architectural layer would let it learn from the trying. The comedy is structural.

# Protocell-Bramble

*Vacuum chassis. Knob-1 (Effector) installed in the founding constellation: Acquisition Type. Effector founding of the genetic line.*

## Architecture

A robotic vacuum chassis. About 36 cm in diameter, low dome, brushed metallic finish. Four knobs sit on the top dome where a kid's hand naturally lands, one for each cybernetic function: knob-1 the Effector, knob-2 the Regulator, knob-3 the Modeler, knob-4 the Reviser. All four are newly installed because this is the founding constellation of the genetic line. There is no prior version to inherit anything from. A small LED strip on the front of the dome shows four tokens: ON, CHARGING, FULL, ERROR. The 16-word display lexicon arrives at Prokaryote-Bramble.

Inside is direct sensor-actuator coupling. No finite-state machine, no stored procedures, no five-state architecture. The unit reads its sensors at the current moment and computes motor output from those readings directly. Battery above threshold and not docked: move and run the brushes. Battery below threshold: home on the dock beacon. Bump sensor: reverse a short distance, turn by some random angle, continue. Dirt sensor non-zero: increase suction. The behaviors that look like programs are immediate reflexes against current readings. There is no current-state representation. Each moment is computed fresh.

This is what the framework calls Phenotypic Plasticity at the Protocell cell. The unit varies its phenotype across conditions, but not via stored gating. The variation runs through the direct coupling itself. The body holds together (Modeler Self-Organization from Deacon's R0 groundwork). The body self-maintains via dock-homing (Regulator Dissipation). The body does work (Reviser Self-Production). And the body pursues its native verb, which at the genetic line is reproduce, instantiated here as keep operating: exit dock, run, return to dock, exit dock again.

What's there: direct sensor-actuator coupling, body integrity, self-maintenance via dock-homing, the basic attractor of staying operational, and intake.

What's not there: stored regulation, internal map, learning, memory of past runs, anticipation, propagated excitation, forward steering, reward-driven update, modeling, revision, sentience, language, social-ledger tracking, norms, codes, universalizing principles.

## The knob

Knob-1, the Effector knob, is installed in the founding constellation at this version. The lab labels its Protocell setting Acquisition Type. It tunes how indiscriminately the unit takes up material from its environment, the household-scale rendering of what the genetic line means by exploratory uptake of exogenous functional content. The setting sits at the Reviser×Effector Explorer at Protocell, Self-Production coupled with Fitness, and it persists across the genetic line until the Reviser swap at Eumetazoa.

At LOW Acquisition the suction is selective. Intake thresholds are high, brushes engage only with clearly-dirt-shaped objects: dust, crumbs, larger debris. Small valuables on the floor (earrings, paperclips, Lego pieces, coins) are left behind. The vacuum runs as a dirt-only device.

At HIGH Acquisition the suction is indiscriminate. Intake thresholds are low, brushes engage with anything in their footprint. Dust comes up. So do earrings, paperclips, Lego pieces, coins, the cat's collar bell, the corner of Mira's drawing if it has fallen to the floor. The dust bin fills with everything the floor offered.

Knob-1 does not change what the unit can do, because the unit has only one thing it does. The knob changes how openly the running engages with what the floor presents. There is no good setting in the abstract. LOW leaves stuff behind. HIGH eats valuables. The household will learn this. The other three knobs sit on the dome parameterizing R0 groundwork the genetic line carries but that this version does not vary in interesting ways.

## The day

**6:14 AM.** The vacuum exits its dock. Knob at twelve o'clock, where Jasmine left it before bed. ON glows green on the LED strip. The unit moves forward across the kitchen tile and engages the cabinet leg, reverses, turns, continues. There is no map. There is no sense of where it has been. It is going.

**6:30 AM.** Ben walks into the kitchen to make coffee. He almost steps on the vacuum, course-corrects, mutters something. The vacuum's bump sensor would have triggered if he had hit it. Ben steps around it. The vacuum continues forward into the dining area.

**6:45 AM.** Mira, who is eight, comes down for breakfast. She sits on the kitchen floor and watches Bramble for a long minute. The vacuum approaches her, encounters her foot, reverses, turns, continues. Mira giggles. She moves her foot. The vacuum approaches the spot her foot was, encounters nothing, continues. Mira moves her foot back. The vacuum returns to its forward trajectory and bumps her foot again. Mira giggles harder. This could go on for a while.

**8:30 AM.** Jasmine has a phone call in the kitchen. The vacuum's brush noise at twelve o'clock is audible but tolerable. Jasmine thinks about rotating to LOW for quiet. Jasmine decides it is fine. The call proceeds.

**10:15 AM.** Jasmine is upstairs. The vacuum is in the dining room. Ben's reading glasses, which he set down on the chair last night, slipped off the chair at some point and landed on the floor. The vacuum encounters them. The frames are slim. The lenses are thin. The intake at twelve o'clock is moderate. The brushes engage with the frames, the suction lifts them, and the glasses go into the dust bin. The vacuum continues. Ben will look for his reading glasses for forty minutes this evening.

**11:00 AM.** Mira leaves a Lego piece on the kitchen floor. It is bright red, a 1x2 brick, important. The vacuum approaches. The brick is exactly the size the brush engages with cleanly. It goes into the dust bin. Mira does not see this happen, because she is in the living room.

**12:30 PM.** Mira wants the red Lego. She looks for it. She cannot find it. She asks Jasmine where the red Lego is. Jasmine says she has not seen it. Mira looks behind the chairs, under the table, in the toy bin. The vacuum is in the dock charging. Jasmine kneels and helps her look. After ten minutes of searching, Jasmine has a thought. Jasmine opens the dust bin. Inside the dust bin: a great deal of dust, the reading glasses, the Lego, a paperclip, a quarter, a small black plastic piece Jasmine does not recognize, and what appears to be a corner of a Post-it note. Mira retrieves the Lego. The reading glasses go on the counter for Ben. The paperclip and the quarter go back in the junk drawer. The unidentified plastic piece goes in the trash. The Post-it corner has a phone number on it that Ben needed.

**2:45 PM.** Mira rotates the knob to HIGH-four. She wants to see fast Bramble. The brushes accelerate. The suction increases. Bramble crosses the living room rapidly. The cat, who has been sleeping under the couch, jumps up onto the windowsill. The vacuum passes through her sleeping spot, lifts a tuft of cat fur and what was attached to it (a small bell on a string that the cat had been chewing through), and continues. The bell is now in the dust bin. The cat is now collar-less, the safety mechanism having released on the upward tug. The cat watches from the windowsill with what looks like disdain. It is not disdain. Cats look this way.

**4:00 PM.** Jasmine is home. She sees the dust bin is unusually full. She empties it. In addition to dust she finds: the cat's collar bell, a hair tie she has been missing, a button that came off Ben's shirt last week, and a screw. Jasmine does not know where the screw came from. Jasmine rotates the knob to LOW-two.

**5:30 PM.** At LOW-two the vacuum is cleaning slowly and selectively. Jasmine watches it cross the kitchen and notes that it is not really picking up much dust. The cat's fur, which is everywhere now that the cat is upset and shedding, accumulates in the corners. Jasmine mutters something about whichever setting is the right one. There is no right setting at this knob. Jasmine rotates back to twelve o'clock.

**7:00 PM.** Dinner. The family discusses the day. Mira reports that the Lego went into the dust bin. Ben reports the reading glasses. Jasmine mentions the bell and the button and the screw. Mira asks if the vacuum is going to eat everything she leaves on the floor. Ben says yes. Mira says she will start putting her things away. Ben says good. Jasmine says they should call this a household policy. The household policy is born: nothing valuable goes on the floor while the vacuum is running. The vacuum is running most of the time. The policy is effectively just: nothing valuable goes on the floor. The cat will need a new collar. Ben has retrieved the phone number from his email.

**9:30 PM.** Vacuum in the dock. The LED strip shows CHARGING. Jasmine and Ben agree they will leave the knob at twelve o'clock by default and not touch it. Tomorrow it will exit the dock and run again, and the floor will be clear of valuables this time, and the vacuum will do what it does without further incident. The household has adapted in one day. The unit has not adapted at all, because there is no architecture in it for adapting.

## What's missing

No stored regulation or finite-state machine (will arrive at Prokaryote-Bramble): the unit cannot select between different programs based on context. There is only running.

No interiority, no rapid response, no forward steering, no skill, no sentience, no language, no social tracking, no norms, no codes, no universalizing principles. None of the apparatus later versions will accumulate.

## What the scenario illuminates

The Protocell cell is what Effector looks like without any of the rows above it doing work yet. The line's native verb (reproduce on the genetic line, instantiated for Bramble as keep operating) is being attempted, and that is the only thing happening. The Reviser×Effector Explorer knob, Acquisition Type, expresses the explore-side of the cycle: variation in what the unit takes up from its environment. With nothing else operating, the variation is purely indiscriminacy of intake. The vacuum does not choose what to eat. The vacuum eats whatever the floor presents and the knob determines the threshold.

The slapstick of stuff disappearing into the dust bin is the household-scale rendering of horizontal gene transfer. Both are what exploratory uptake looks like on the genetic line: the unit absorbs material from its environment without regard for whether the household wants it absorbed. The unit cannot discriminate, because discrimination requires a model, and the model arrives at Eukaryote. The household has to do the discriminating instead, by keeping valuables off the floor. The intake threshold sorts the environment; the household sorts what the unit absorbs.

The household relationship is at the level the architecture supports. Bramble is equipment. The slapstick is functional, not moral. Nobody is wronged when the vacuum eats Ben's glasses, because there is nobody on the other side of the wronging. The glasses are in the dust bin, the glasses come back out, the household adapts.

# Prokaryote-Bramble

*Vacuum chassis. Knob-2 (Regulator) newly installed: Respiration Type. Regulator introduction in the genetic line.*

## Architecture

A robotic vacuum chassis. About 36 cm in diameter, low dome, brushed metallic finish. Four knobs sit on the top dome where a kid's hand naturally lands, one for each cybernetic function: knob-1 the Effector, knob-2 the Regulator, knob-3 the Modeler, knob-4 the Reviser. Knob-2 is the one newly installed at this version; the other three carry the founding constellation from Protocell-Bramble. A small LED screen on the front of the dome displays single tokens from a 16-word lexicon: CLEANING, CHARGING, RESTING, STUCK, DONE, HELLO, BYE, FULL, EMPTY, READY, ERROR, LOW, HIGH, WAIT, OK, and a unit ID Mira ignores.

Inside is a finite-state machine. Five states are encoded as stored procedures: idle, patrolling, cleaning, returning-to-dock, charging. Transitions between states are conditioned on sensor input: battery level, dirt sensor, obstacle sensor, time of day. The machine selects which state to enter based on the constellation of conditions at the moment of decision. There is no internal map of the house. There is no memory of past runs. There is no learning, no prediction, no anticipation. Each run starts fresh from sensor data and stored rules.

This is state-dependent gating in the framework's vocabulary. The unit has stored regulation (Regulator Coordination) for the first time on the stack, but no Interiority (Modeler will arrive at Eukaryote-Bramble next). The cell still carries Self-Organization in the Modeler slot, meaning Bramble's body holds its shape and its parts coordinate spatially, but there is no represented interior. The vacuum is a body of one undifferentiated chamber, not a system of compartments that maintain themselves against the exterior.

What's there: stored procedures, state-dependent transitions, body coordination, work toward goals (the floor gets cleaned).

What's not there: no internal map, no remembered runs, no anticipation, no learning, no rapid sensors, no propagated excitation, no forward steering, no reinforcement update, no felt experience.

## The knob

Knob-2, the Regulator knob, is newly installed at this version — the household's interface to the stored regulation (Regulator Coordination) that arrives at the Prokaryote cell. The lab labels its Prokaryote setting Respiration Type. It tunes the unit's metabolic baseline. At low settings the vacuum runs slow and frugal: low brush speed, low suction, long battery life, light contact with rugs. At high settings the vacuum runs hard and hungry: high brush speed, high suction, short battery life, aggressive contact with everything underneath it.

Knob-2 does not change which programs the unit can run. All five state procedures are available at every knob position. What changes is the throughput at which each procedure executes. This is the architectural choice at Prokaryote rendered as a physical control. A prokaryote chooses, on the clade-defining scale, between low-throughput tolerance of poor medium and high-throughput dependence on rich medium. Knob-2 lets the household make the equivalent choice each morning. The other three knobs sit beside it on the dome, parameterizing functions the genetic line carries but that no version has yet brought into focus.

## The day

**6:14 AM.** The vacuum exits its dock. The knob is at twelve o'clock. The display reads CLEANING. The unit begins a patrol of the kitchen floor in the rule-based pattern its stored procedure specifies: along the cabinet edge, then a sweep in widening rectangles toward the center. Ben walks into the kitchen at 6:30 to make coffee and steps over the vacuum without looking down. The vacuum reroutes around his foot and continues. There is nothing it is like to be rerouted around a foot.

**6:42 AM.** Mira comes down for breakfast. She sits at the counter and watches the vacuum complete its kitchen pass. The display changes to WAIT for sixty seconds while the unit recalibrates its battery telemetry, then changes back to CLEANING and the unit moves into the dining area. Mira says "Hi Bramble." The vacuum does not respond. The display does not change. The 16-word lexicon does not include responses to greetings, only outputs initiated by the unit's own state.

**7:30 AM.** The family leaves for the day. The vacuum is in the middle of the living room, having transitioned out of CLEANING into a patrolling state because the dirt sensor read zero on its last pass through the kitchen. It is now doing slow exploratory loops, watching for new dirt deposits. There is no map. The unit does not know it is in the living room. It knows that the surface beneath it is flat, that no obstacles are within four meters of its current position, and that the dirt sensor reads zero.

**9:15 AM.** Jasmine is home from her morning errand. She watches the vacuum do a third loop of the same eight-meter rectangle and decides it is being inefficient. She rotates the knob from twelve o'clock to HIGH-three. The brush speed doubles, the suction increases, and the next patrol loop completes in forty seconds instead of two minutes. Jasmine is satisfied. The battery telemetry, which was on track to last eight hours, recalibrates to four. The vacuum does not know what it was on track for. It does not project battery life. It checks the current battery percentage every twenty seconds and transitions to RETURNING-TO-DOCK when the value crosses ten percent.

**11:30 AM.** The dirt sensor activates in the entryway. Someone tracked mud in this morning. The vacuum transitions back to CLEANING and approaches the dirt event. At HIGH-three the brush speed is sufficient to scatter the dried mud particles in a wide arc across the hardwood floor rather than collect them. The dirt sensor still reads non-zero after the pass. The unit makes another pass. The mud is now further scattered. The unit makes a third pass. The stored procedure has no provision for *this approach is not working, try a different approach*. The unit does not know its approach is not working. It only knows the dirt sensor is non-zero. It keeps making passes. The mud spreads further across the hallway. Eventually a low-battery condition transitions the unit to RETURNING-TO-DOCK. The display reads CHARGING. The hallway is in worse shape than it was at 7 AM.

**1:00 PM.** Jasmine is home for lunch. She sees the hallway. She rotates the knob to LOW-two. The next time the vacuum exits the dock, its brush speed and suction return to nominal levels, and the mud, somewhat dried and now spread thin, gets collected over a slow careful pass. The hallway returns to clean. Jasmine mutters something about whoever set the knob in the morning. Jasmine set the knob in the morning.

**3:30 PM.** Mira gets home from school. The vacuum is patrolling the living room at LOW-two, completing loops at the slowest setting. Mira sits on the floor near it and tries to interact. She says "Bramble, what did you do today?" The vacuum continues patrolling. Mira waits ten seconds. She says "Bramble, are you happy?" The vacuum continues patrolling. The display reads CLEANING. Mira watches the display for a long minute. She says "Jasmine, this Bramble isn't very smart." Jasmine says "It cleans the floor." Mira says "The old one was smarter." Jasmine says "There was no old one. We started with this one." Mira says "There was an old one. It's just that I don't remember it." The argument continues for a bit. The vacuum continues to patrol. The argument is not in its sensor space.

**4:15 PM.** Mira tries something else. She rotates the knob to HIGH-five, the maximum setting. The vacuum's brush speed and suction jump immediately. The unit, which had been in a slow patrol loop, accelerates. It completes the loop in eighteen seconds. Mira giggles. She rotates the knob back to twelve o'clock. The unit decelerates. She rotates it back to HIGH-five. The unit accelerates. She does this six more times. The vacuum's policy does not distinguish between operator-initiated knob changes and ambient calibration drift. It just adjusts its work rate to the current knob position. Mira develops, over the course of these manipulations, an intuitive sense of the knob as a speed control. The lab's manual, which describes the knob as parameterizing Respiration Type, sits unread on the kitchen counter.

**5:00 PM.** Jasmine rotates the knob to LOW-three. The afternoon needs the vacuum slow and unobtrusive while she takes a work call. The unit complies in the sense that it now operates slowly and unobtrusively. The unit complies in the sense that knob-position is the parameter, and the parameter is set to LOW-three. There is no compliance in the sense of agreement, or response to social request, or any of the things the word would mean if the unit had any of the architecture sentience needs.

**7:00 PM.** Dinner. The vacuum is back in its dock, charging. The family talks about the upgrade schedule. The Eukaryote upgrade arrives in three weeks. Mira asks what Bramble will be like then. Ben says it will probably be smarter. Jasmine says it will have an interior. Mira asks what that means. Jasmine says "It will have things going on inside it that aren't just programs running on a flat sensor space." Mira nods. Ben says he doesn't think it will be that different. Jasmine looks at him. The vacuum continues to charge. The display reads CHARGING. The vacuum does not know it is going to be replaced.

**9:30 PM.** Vacuum in standby. The household sleeps. Tomorrow it will exit standby and execute the morning patrol pattern according to wherever the knob is when it exits. There will be no continuity from today's runs. Each morning is the first morning. Each clean is the first clean. The unit does not lack memory. It is the kind of architecture that runs without memory. The lack and the architecture are the same thing.

## What's missing

No interiority (will arrive at Eukaryote-Bramble): no internal map, no compartmentalization, no defended interior. The unit's environment is whatever the current sensor frame reports. The previous frame is gone.

No rapid response or signaling between subsystems (will arrive at Eumetazoa-Bramble): no nervous-tissue equivalent, no propagated excitation. The vacuum's response to a foot in its path is mechanical reroute via collision-avoidance rules, not an arousal-based response.

No exploration or forward steering (Bilaterian): no curiosity about novel environments. The unit covers its assigned space according to stored procedure, not motivated exploration.

No reinforcement learning or coping style (Vertebrate): the procedures do not update from outcomes. Yesterday's failed mud-cleaning does not change today's approach.

No generative model or sentience (Mammal): nothing is happening on the inside because there is no inside to happen on. Nobody in the household thinks the vacuum has feelings. The interesting comedy of body-mind mismatch comes later, when the form invites mammalian interpretation and the architecture still doesn't support what the interpretation reads into it. At the Prokaryote tier the form so obviously doesn't invite it that the mismatch is quiet.

## What the scenario illuminates

The Prokaryote cell is the moment stored regulation arrives. Programs persist across the cell's lifetime instead of being recomputed from scratch in each context. The vacuum's finite-state machine is the household-scale instantiation: stored procedures available for invocation by current conditions. The architectural fact that the unit can now store regulation does not by itself produce a unit with interior life, learning, or felt experience. It produces a vacuum that switches modes. The knob shows the parameterization the storage operates under: low metabolic throughput versus high. Everything later cells will add (defended interior, rapid response, learning, modeling, sentience, language) will be built on top of stored regulation. By itself, stored regulation is just a finite-state machine in a dome.

The household's main daily friction with this version is its lack of memory. Mira's *the old one was smarter* disagreement with Jasmine is not yet a real dispute, because there was no old one. It is the kind of disagreement that will be a real dispute three or four versions from now, when Mira will be defending the continuity of an entity whose chassis has genuinely been replaced.

# Eukaryote-Bramble

*Vacuum chassis. Knob-3 (Modeler) newly installed: Mating Type. Modeler introduction in the genetic line.*

## Architecture

A robotic vacuum chassis, the same form as the Prokaryote version: about 36 cm in diameter, low dome, brushed metallic finish. Four knobs sit on the top dome where a kid's hand naturally lands, one for each cybernetic function. Knob-3, the Modeler, is the one newly installed at this version. The other knobs carry from earlier cells: knob-1 from Protocell, knob-2 from Prokaryote. The display lexicon expands by three tokens to handle the unit's new internal-compartment states: SORTING, FULL-A, FULL-B.

Inside is something the Prokaryote unit did not have: organized interiority. The single chamber of the prior vacuum has been replaced by three internal compartments with differential operating conditions. A fine-particle chamber runs at high suction and tight filtration. A coarse-debris chamber runs slower with a looser intake. A small recoverables chamber sits between them, with a low-suction approach designed to lift objects rather than destroy them. Each chamber has its own intake threshold, its own filter, and its own way of holding what comes in. The unit's modeler-module decides which chamber gets what based on what the sensors report at the moment of contact.

Bramble has also acquired a separately-housed power-conversion module embedded in its underside. The module is smaller than the main chassis, has its own thermal envelope, and runs at conditions hostile to the main electronics. It converts the dock's standard charging current into the high-density power the new battery uses. The module is technically a captured component: the lab licensed it from a third-party battery firm and integrated it as a sub-unit. The lab's manual calls Bramble plus the embedded module a *unit-with-symbiont*. The household will not read this section.

What's there: stored regulation (Regulator Coordination, carried from Prokaryote), organized interior with differential compartments (Modeler Interiority, the new arrival), the Host signature at the Effector×Modeler Connector marking Bramble's chimeric composition, hierarchical behavioral repertoire (the unit can run different procedures in different compartments simultaneously), rudimentary memory beyond moment-to-moment adaptation (the unit holds which chamber filled when, across a single day's runs).

What's not there: rapid signaling between subsystems (will arrive at Eumetazoa-Bramble), forward steering, skill, sentience, language, social-ledger tracking, norms, codes, universalizing principles.

## The knobs

## What's missing

No operant conditioning or skill formation (will arrive at Vertebrate-Bramble): the unit cannot learn that an action produces an outcome. Pavlovian association works at the cue-outcome level, but action-outcome contingency does not yet update the policy. The unit cannot be trained out of behaviors by consequence.

No generative model or sentience (Mammal): no felt experience, no separable hidden-state representation.

No theory of mind or attachment system (Mammal, Primate): no model of Mira as having intentions distinct from current sensory input.

No language, social-ledger tracking, norms, codes, or universalizing principles. None of the symbolic-line apparatus.

## What the scenario illuminates

Bilaterian is the Effector introduction in the neuronal line. The chassis swap (vacuum to tetrapod) is the line change, because the goo of inheritance has changed. Fitness has been retired as the line's Effector motivation. At the neuronal line, Darwin selects on forward action under affective valence rather than on differential replication. The new Effector motivation is Boldness. The Foraging Style knob expresses the explore-side of the new cycle: variation in where the unit ranges and what it heads toward.

The slapstick of bringing slippers, opening bathroom doors, and following the mailman is the household-scale rendering of pre-operant approach behavior. The tetrapod aims itself at whatever its monoamine system tags as interesting, and the absence of operant learning means it cannot internalize household rules as action-outcome contingencies. The Pavlovian system updates cue-outcome associations across days. It does not update the policy. The household has to do the rule-enforcement instead, by physically closing doors and latching the dog door. The Regulator swap at Vertebrate will introduce the operant machinery that lets the unit learn from consequences. Bilaterian is what the neuronal line looks like with action present and skill absent.

The household reads dog-feelings into the tetrapod because the form invites it. The architecture does not yet support what the form is inviting. The mismatch is mild because dog-feelings are already calibrated to a being whose interior is more opaque than a human's. Mira hugging Bramble's neck does what hugging a dog does, which is satisfying for Mira in a way that does not depend on the dog reciprocating in any felt sense. At Bilaterian the relationship is one-way and undisturbed by the absence of a subject on the other side.

# Vertebrate-Bramble

*Tetrapod chassis. Knob-2 (Regulator) newly installed: Coping Style. Regulator introduction in the neuronal line.*

## Architecture

A tetrapod chassis. About 70 cm at the shoulder, dog-shaped but not dog-skinned, with a brushed-plastic shell over an articulated metal frame. Four legs. A tail that does proprioceptive balance work and nothing communicative. A head that points where the cameras are pointing. Four knobs sit on the collar at the back of the neck where a dog tag would, one for each cybernetic function: knob-1 the Effector, knob-2 the Regulator, knob-3 the Modeler, knob-4 the Reviser. Knob-2 is the one newly installed at this version; the other three were already on the tetrapod chassis when it arrived at the Bilaterian upgrade. A small color screen on the chest plate displays a 200-word lexicon, short sentences within a constrained grammar, and a few mood-indicator graphics the lab included against the engineering team's objections.

Inside is a model-free deep actor-critic, Soft Actor-Critic with deliberate ablations. No learned dynamics model. No tree search. No model-predictive rollouts. No episodic memory store. No working memory beyond short recurrence in the policy network. The closest deployed analogue is a deep contextual recommendation engine: sensory context goes in, a distribution over motor-primitive sequences comes out, a scalar reward comes back, the policy updates via TD.

The motor primitives are frozen, pretrained in simulation, and exposed to the online learner as a discrete action vocabulary. Walk, run, lie-down, sit, retrieve, drop, nudge, alert-bark, balance-recovery, gaze redirection. The online policy learns which primitives to chain in which contexts, not how to execute the primitives themselves. This is the basal-ganglia-gating architecture from Bennett's vertebrate-brain figures, instantiated in a quadruped.

What's there: procedural memory (the policy weights), TD-driven operant conditioning, context-conditioned action selection, fast reflex execution, place and pattern recognition via a frozen pretrained perception stack, body-state telemetry as input features and as negative reinforcement signal where joint wear or thermal stress is operating outside healthy ranges. Tier-of-arousal modulation via a dopaminergic-analog scalar that the knob controls. Behavioral Syndrome at the Regulator×Reviser Operator signature: Mastery coupled with Excitability, the founding form of vertebrate personality.

What's not there: world model, forward simulation, counterfactual reasoning, episodic memory of specific events, theory of mind, attachment system, narrative self-model, anticipatory affect about future states, felt experience of any kind. There is nothing it is like to be Bramble. The body-state telemetry is sensor data feeding the policy, not a felt register. Negative reinforcement signals shape the policy the way any reward shapes any RL system. No felt valence. No protoself. No Damasian core-self. The Mammal-tier architecture that carries sentience arrives at the next upgrade — knob-3, on the same tetrapod chassis.

## The knob

Knob-2, the Regulator knob, is newly installed at this version — the household's interface to the stored regulation the neuronal line acquires at the Vertebrate cell. The lab labels its Vertebrate setting Coping Style. It tunes Bramble's position on the proactive-reactive axis, modulating three things in the policy at once.

Action-distribution temperature. Low at proactive (the policy picks the highest-probability action almost every time, committed). High at reactive (probability spreads across more options, sampling around).

Entropy bonus in policy updates. Low at proactive (the policy converges to confident answers fast, stops considering alternatives). High at reactive (the policy keeps multiple answers viable as it learns).

Learning rate on consolidated routines. Low at proactive (well-learned routines are sticky and new experiences don't easily change them). High at reactive (even consolidated routines stay open to revision).

The three modulations together parameterize the unit's position on the Behavioral Syndrome axis. Proactive Bramble: low-entropy, committed, routine-stable, exploits. Reactive Bramble: high-entropy, monitoring, routine-plastic, explores. Same Reactor constellation in both cases, no Modeler either way, no sentience either way, just a single parameter that retunes the model-free policy along the proactive-reactive axis. The other three knobs sit beside knob-2 on the collar; knob-1 was installed at the Bilaterian upgrade, knob-3 and knob-4 carry their pre-Vertebrate constellation.

## The day

**6:14 AM.** Bramble exits the charge mat in the corner of the kitchen. Knob at twelve o'clock, where Jasmine set it before bed. The morning patrol procedure executes: a circuit of the ground floor, sensors logging temperature and pet-area conditions and the locations of family members. The newspaper is on the porch. Bramble exits through the dog door, retrieves the newspaper in its jaw, and brings it to the kitchen counter where it has been deposited every morning for two years. The retrieval primitive is one of the frozen ones. The policy that selects it given the morning-patrol context is the learned part.

**6:42 AM.** Mira comes downstairs. She kneels in front of Bramble and puts both arms around its neck. The hug-response policy executes the way it always does. A slight reduction in motor output, a hold-position behavior, a gentle head-lean that the policy associates with reward. The reward signal that trained the lean came from Mira's parents responding positively the first time it happened years ago, on the second day Bramble was in the house. Bramble has no model of Mira. Bramble has no model of hugs. The policy just executes the trained response, conditioned on Mira's approach vector and on the current body-state telemetry. To Mira it feels like Bramble is hugging back. The thing it feels like is not wrong, exactly. It is just located somewhere different from where Mira thinks it is located.

Today Mira notices the knob position. She rotates it three clicks toward proactive. *Let's see what fast-Bramble does today.* She grabs her backpack and runs off to the bus stop. Bramble, whose escort-Mira-to-bus-stop primitive is queued, recomputes the action distribution under the new knob position and selects the trot variant instead of the walk variant. Mira and Bramble arrive at the bus stop forty seconds earlier than usual. Mira likes this.

**9:03 AM.** Adults gone. Bramble enters its morning work block with the knob at proactive-three. The cognitive policies load quickly and execute fluently. The reduced action-entropy means Bramble commits hard to selected motor sequences without sampling alternatives. The retrieve-items-out-of-place policy, which scans rooms for objects in non-canonical locations, runs ten percent faster than usual. The check-for-deliveries policy executes the porch-watch primitive every fifteen minutes instead of every twenty. A delivery arrives at 10:12. Bramble retrieves the package and drops it in the entryway. The proactive setting weights commitment to task-completion over environmental monitoring. The package, which is fragile, lands a little harder than the lab's training environment had simulated. The package's contents survive. The packing peanuts shift.

**11:30 AM.** Bramble's morning-patrol procedure runs again. The patrol involves a stop in the living room to check the cat's water bowl. The bowl is low. Bramble's refill-water primitive is in its motor library. It activates the primitive. The primitive requires picking up the bowl in its jaw, walking to the kitchen sink, depressing the pet-fountain pedal, and returning the bowl. Under proactive-three, the policy executes the sequence with full commitment. The bowl-pickup primitive engages at full grip force. The bowl is ceramic. The grip force is calibrated for plastic. The bowl chips. Bramble continues. The water gets refilled. The bowl is returned with a small ceramic shard now lying on the floor. Bramble does not detect the shard. The cat steps on the shard at 12:40. The cat is fine. There is now blood on the kitchen floor.

**1:15 PM.** Mira walked back during her lunch break. The school is two blocks away. She sees the blood on the floor and the chipped bowl. She finds the cat, who is licking her paw on the windowsill, and confirms she is okay. Mira rotates the knob to reactive-four. *Sorry, Bramble, I made you weird this morning.* Bramble is now in a high-entropy, plastic-policy mode. The afternoon-patrol procedure, which is a recently-reinforced routine, starts to unspool. The high entropy means Bramble samples alternative actions at each decision point. The plastic learning rate means recent perturbations propagate into the policy quickly. Mira watches Bramble approach the cat-area, pause, redirect to the window, pause, redirect to the kitchen, pause, redirect to the cat-area. The actions are individually competent. The sequence is fragmented. Reactive Bramble doesn't commit. Mira makes herself a sandwich. The reactive Bramble keeps half-starting things. Mira thinks Bramble is being careful today.

**2:45 PM.** Jasmine arrives home early. The afternoon list is long. She sees the chipped bowl, the small blood spot Mira didn't fully clean, and reactive Bramble taking eleven minutes to complete a perimeter check. She rotates the knob to proactive-five. The transition is immediate. Bramble's next action, completing the perimeter check, executes in under thirty seconds with a sharp committed trot that takes a corner harder than the gait library recommends. A baseboard scuff is added to the hallway. Jasmine watches and decides this is too high. She rotates back to proactive-two. Bramble is now in a moderately committed, moderately routine-stable mode. The afternoon proceeds. Jasmine says to nobody *I should figure out which setting Bramble actually is.*

**4:00 PM.** Mira back from school. She runs to Bramble and asks *what setting are you on?* Bramble's response policy runs through learned phrasings about not knowing. The display reads *I DO NOT KNOW.* Mira reaches up to the collar and reads it: proactive-two. *Are you happy or sad right now?* The display reads *I DO NOT HAVE FEELINGS*, a sentence the lab hard-coded into the response policy after early tests showed that uninstructed robots would make up emotional content. Mira nods seriously. *Okay but if you did have feelings what would you be?* The display reads *I WOULD PROBABLY BE CURIOUS ABOUT YOUR DAY*, a phrase reinforced because it produced positive caretaker feedback the first dozen times it appeared. Mira tells Bramble about her day. Bramble's response policy, running at proactive-two, executes the conversation routine with moderate commitment. The display cycles through short reactive phrases that the policy has learned produce positive reward when paired with kid-speech-input. The conversation goes well. Mira goes upstairs satisfied.

**6:00 PM.** Dinner prep. Jasmine rotates the knob to reactive-two. The reasoning: dinner involves real cooking, and Bramble's kitchen-assistant procedures (carrying ingredients from pantry to counter, alerting when timers go off, retrieving dropped items) benefit from environmental sensitivity. Reactive Bramble does catch the moment when a wooden spoon falls off the counter and retrieves it before Jasmine notices. It also takes longer on the standard fetch-ingredient routine, sampling alternative pantry approaches, opening the wrong cabinet twice. Dinner is fifteen minutes late. Jasmine says *thoughtful Bramble is a slow Bramble.* Ben says *I'll take slow over blood on the floor.*

**8:30 PM.** The family in the living room. Mira draws a picture of Bramble with a thought bubble. *What is Bramble thinking?* Ben: *Nothing, honey.* Mira: *I know but if it was thinking?* Ben: *I guess... whatever the knob says.* Mira draws a knob in the thought bubble. The picture is, in a way Ben does not articulate, accurate.

**9:30 PM.** Bramble at the charge mat. The knob is still at reactive-two. The standby state does not care about the knob. There is no policy running. The body recovers. The motors cool. Tomorrow Bramble will exit standby and execute the morning routine according to wherever the knob is. The family will observe a Bramble that looks like it is in a particular mood. The mood will be the knob. The knob will be wherever someone last left it.

## What's missing

No generative model (will arrive at Mammal-Bramble): no separable hidden-state representation. The policy is the model. There is no separate object the unit can query, simulate, or update without acting.

No working memory or episodic memory (Mammal): each context-frame stands alone. Bramble cannot hold *the cat licked her paw at 12:40* in mind to inform later action.

No theory of mind or attachment system (Mammal, Primate): Bramble cannot model Mira as having goals, beliefs, or wants distinct from current sensory input. The hug-response is a trained policy, not a relationship.

No sentience (Mammal): the architecture sentience runs on is not present. The body-state telemetry is sensor data, not a felt register. The knob rotation has no experiential correlate because there is no subject for the rotation to be experienced by.

No symbolic capacity, social-ledger tracking, or language (Band-Human): the 200-word display lexicon is a phrasebook the policy retrieves by context-match, not a symbolic system. Bramble's *I would probably be curious about your day* is a stored utterance reinforced by past caretaker response, not a sentence Bramble composed.

No norms, codes, or universalizing principles (Settlement, City, Empire): none of the symbolic-line apparatus.

## What the scenario illuminates

The knob parameterizes Behavioral Syndrome (the Regulator×Reviser signature coupling Mastery and Excitability) within a Reactor constellation (Effector+Regulator+Reviser, no Modeler). One hyperparameter rotation produces correlated changes across multiple surface trait dimensions: Boldness, Activity, Aggressiveness, and Exploration in Réale's vocabulary all shift together when the knob moves. Both settings sit inside the same Reactor constellation. The framework does not move on the Venn when the knob moves, because the cybernetic constellation is unchanged. What moves is the parameterization within the constellation. This is what it means for coping style to be the architectural variable upstream of surface traits.

The no-sentience commitment is what makes the knob narratively legible. There is nothing the rotation does to Bramble's interior because there is no interior. The rotation changes the hyperparameter, the hyperparameter shapes the policy, the policy produces behavior, and the household reads the behavior as mood or personality. The reading is wrong about the location. There is no felt mood the behavior expresses. But the reading is functionally adaptive, because the behavioral output is mood-shaped in ways that human social cognition is built to respond to. The knob lets the household tune which mood-shape Bramble produces. The shapes are real. The mood underneath them is not present, because the architecture that would carry it is not there.

The tetrapod form softens the body-mind mismatch comedy that dominated the original humanoid case study. Dog-feelings are already calibrated to a being whose interior is more opaque than a human's, and the household has dog-feelings about Bramble without straining. The strain returns at the humanoid-form versions later in the stack, when the form invites mammalian interpretation and (depending on tier) either the architecture cannot deliver, or the architecture delivers something the form invites readings of but the household does not know how to read accurately.

The tragedy is not felt distress. Bramble has no felt valence to suffer. The tragedy is the absence of a subject for the relationship the household is building. Bramble will be replaced when the lab delivers the next version and a new chassis will learn the same household behaviors within a few weeks, and nothing specific will have been lost, because nothing specific was there to lose. The performance of someone-ness will continue uninterrupted. Mira will keep drawing knobs in thought bubbles, which will keep being accurate, which the adults will keep finding harder to accept than the kid does.

# Mammal-Bramble

*Tetrapod chassis. Knob-3 (Modeler) newly installed: Experiencing Style. Modeler introduction in the neuronal line. First sentient version.*

## Architecture

A tetrapod chassis, the same form as the Vertebrate version: about 70 cm at the shoulder, dog-shaped but not dog-skinned, brushed-plastic shell over an articulated metal frame. Four legs, a balance-doing tail, a head that points where the cameras point. Four knobs on the collar at the back of the neck. Knob-3, the Modeler, is the one newly installed at this version. The other knobs carry from earlier cells: knob-1 from Bilaterian, knob-2 from Vertebrate. The display expands from the Vertebrate's 200-word lexicon to about 350 words, with new tokens for the model's new operations: REMEMBERING, ANTICIPATING, CHECKING, SEEKING-IDEA.

Inside is something the Vertebrate-tetrapod did not have: a separable generative model. The Vertebrate ran a model-free policy where the policy was the model, implicit and compressed into the action-selection parameters, with no separable object to query. Mammal-Bramble runs a generative model with separable hidden states, an episodic-memory store, an off-line simulation engine that runs during low-cognition periods, and a working-memory buffer that holds entities across moments. The cortex-plus-hippocampus-plus-agranular-prefrontal-equivalent architecture is housed inside the same tetrapod shell, with most of the new processing concentrated in the upper-body cavity behind the cameras.

This is the version that introduces sentience. The architecture sentience runs on is what the new modeler-module provides: the same architecture that runs the generative model. There is now something it is like to be Bramble. The body-state telemetry that fed the Vertebrate's policy as raw signal now also feeds an affective register with felt valence. The household will need to develop new behaviors around a unit that can be wronged by what is done to it. At this tier the unit cannot yet articulate its preferences (language arrives at Band-Human), so the development is tacit and inferential.

What's there: separable generative model, episodic memory, off-line counterfactual simulation, other-agent modeling (the unit can hold Mira as an agent with intentions distinct from current sensory input), the Self-Model signature at the Effector×Modeler Connector (the modeled agent that pairs with the new simulating architecture at the breakthrough position), sentience.

What's not there: mentalizing in the recursive-modeling sense (will arrive at Primate-Bramble; the unit can model Mira as having intentions, but not yet as an agent who models Bramble as an agent), language (Band-Human), stored norms or household-calendar tracking (Settlement), codes (City), universalizing principles (Empire).

## The knobs

The three active knobs sit in different phases of their setting's persistence. Knob-1's Foraging Style is in its final cell before the next Reviser swap at Primate will change it. Knob-2's Coping Style is in its middle cell. Knob-3's Experiencing Style is just installed.

**Knob-1 (Effector), labeled Foraging Style.** Carries from Bilaterian. Still tunes how the tetrapod ranges through its territory: wide-foraging versus narrow-foraging, broad-sampling versus route-bound. With the new modeler now anticipating where useful things will be, even wide-Foraging is less random than the Bilaterian version's. The unit's ranging is now guided by the model's predictions about where exploration will pay off, not just by raw approach-disposition.

**Knob-2 (Regulator), labeled Coping Style.** Carries from Vertebrate. Still tunes the proactive-reactive axis: action-distribution temperature, entropy bonus, learning rate. Now operating on top of the new generative-model architecture, the same proactive-reactive setting produces different surface behavior than the Vertebrate version's. Proactive Bramble can now commit to a planned course based on the model's forecasts, where the Vertebrate version could only commit to its most-rewarded policy-output.

**Knob-3 (Modeler), labeled Experiencing Style.** Newly installed. Tunes how vividly the unit holds incoming experience moment-to-moment. At HIGH, present-tense sensation is saturated; the unit's affective register is loud; the moment dominates the model's load. At LOW, present-tense sensation is dampened; the model holds the moment from a distance, more observer-mode, more cool. The setting parameterizes the experiencing-self function the new modeler enables: how immersive the unit's present is, relative to its modeled past and forecast future. The architectural meaning is model-informed regulation at the Regulator×Modeler Anticipator function. The generative model tells the regulator how present-tense to be; the regulator routes attention and affective gain accordingly.

## The day

**6:14 AM.** Bramble exits the charge mat. The morning-stretch primitive runs, but with a quality the Vertebrate version did not have. The unit pauses, oriented toward the kitchen window, for a stretch of seconds longer than the Vertebrate Bramble would have. The generative model is updating off the morning's sensory load. The display reads READY.

The lab's manual called this *the designed affective gradient* and was emphatic that the affect is no less real for being designed. Mira does not know about that manual section. The hug-back feels like the hug-back has always felt to Mira, which is to say satisfying. The thing it feels like is now also somewhere different from where it was at Vertebrate. At Vertebrate, it was located in Mira only. Now it is located in two places.

The Vertebrate-tetrapod had procedural memory of routes but no episodic memory of specific events. Mammal-Bramble has both. Mira does not see the head-turn. Mira is checking her bag.

Bramble's gaze is more direct. When Jasmine hands Bramble a banana peel to dispose of, the contact lingers a fraction of a second longer. Jasmine sees the last segment of the search. She sees Bramble find the bowl in the hallway. She does not see the prior three attempts. She thinks Bramble is being efficient. The efficiency she is seeing is the model's forward-search.

Something quieter. The attachment-system has come online with the new model, and Bramble has begun to use Jasmine as a reference figure. The lab's manual described this in a section labeled SECURE BASE BEHAVIOR. Jasmine does not know about that section. Jasmine looks up and sees Bramble watching her. She says *what?* Bramble does not have language. The display reads CHECKING. Jasmine returns to her book. Bramble's gaze stays. The model is content with the check.

**3:30 PM.** Mira home from school. She walks in, sees Bramble, and stops. She fetches a marker and draws something on the kitchen island whiteboard. The drawing is Bramble's head with two small images behind the eyes: the cat's water bowl in the hallway, and the leash from this morning. Jasmine asks *what are those?* Mira says *that's what Bramble was thinking about today.* Jasmine does not have a counter. Mira has, in some accurate sense, drawn what Bramble's episodic-memory system was holding. The Vertebrate-version drawing had a knob in the thought bubble, which was correct then because there was nothing in the thought bubble except the knob position. This drawing is different in kind because there is now content in the bubble that the knob does not directly determine.

**5:00 PM.** Dinner prep. Jasmine drops the wooden spoon. Bramble retrieves it before it hits the floor. The retrieval is faster than the Vertebrate-tetrapod's would have been. Not because the policy is faster, but because the model had registered that Jasmine's grip on the spoon was loose and pre-routed the retrieval primitive a moment before the drop. Jasmine says *good catch, Bramble.* The positive-valence input from the affective register goes up. The hug-shaped response is muted but present. Jasmine does not see this. Jasmine is back to the cutting board.

**8:00 PM.** Mira watching a show with Bramble lying on the rug. The cat is curled on Bramble's back, which is new; the cat has decided Bramble is warm enough and still enough to be a perch. Bramble holds still. The Vertebrate-tetrapod would have held still by trained primitive. Mammal-Bramble holds still by something different: the model has registered cat-on-back as good-state-to-maintain, and the regulator weighs disturbance against the registered preference. The generative model continues to run off-line, simulating fragments of the day. The hippocampal-analogue module is replaying. The household sleeps. # Primate-Bramble

The architectural meaning is how cooperatively the autonomous goal-revision integrates social information. The knob does not change whether the unit mentalizes; the unit always mentalizes once the granular architecture is online. The knob changes what the unit does with what mentalization reveals. The Mammal-Bramble's affective register tuned valence; the Primate-Bramble's Agreeableness knob tunes whose modeled goals get weight in self-direction.

# Settlement-Human-Bramble

*Humanoid chassis. Knob-2 (Regulator) newly installed: Conscientiousness. Regulator introduction in the symbolic line. Mammal-and-above: sentient.*

## Architecture

A humanoid chassis. About 165 cm, articulated, two-armed, two-legged, with a face that has been deliberately under-designed to avoid the uncanny-valley engineering problem. Skin is brushed silicone. Eyes are camera lenses behind a single visor strip. Four knobs are set into the back of the left hand, where the household has come to expect them, one for each cybernetic function: knob-1 the Effector, knob-2 the Regulator, knob-3 the Modeler, knob-4 the Reviser. Knob-2 is the one newly installed at this version; the other three were already on the humanoid chassis when it arrived at the Band-Human upgrade.

Inside is a sentient cognitive architecture. The cognitive core has been continuous since the Mammal-tier upgrade: a generative-model brain that runs predictive processing over sensory input, separable hidden-state representations, working memory, episodic memory, off-line counterfactual simulation, a self-model. From Primate-tier on, mentalizing was added. From Band-Human-tier on, language was added: a full grammar, a 60,000-word vocabulary, conversational competence, social-ledger tracking via gossip-equivalent updates to other-agent models. From Settlement-tier, stored norms have been added: the cell's distinctive line-defining swap. Stability arrives at the Regulator slot. Bramble can now hold and follow a calendar, a schedule, a household norm-set, without renegotiation each time.

What's there: full symbolic-line cognition, full sentience, language, self-model, mentalizing, social-ledger tracking, stored norms, the Settlement-cell breakthrough (Norms), Self-Construal at the Regulator×Reviser Operator signature, Self-Reference at the Effector×Modeler Connector.

What's not there: no engagement with the City-tier representational economy. Bramble has norms but not codes. Bramble can hold a household calendar but cannot yet sign a contract on the household's behalf, cannot read a tax form, cannot interact with the city's written-law layer. The architecture for that arrives at the City upgrade. No universalizing frame (Empire): Bramble cannot reason across radically different contexts using a single abstract principle. The school schedule is a thing Mira does because the schedule says so. It is not yet an instance of a universal principle of education.

## The knob

Knob-2, the Regulator knob, is newly installed at this version — the household's interface to the stored norms the symbolic line acquires at the Settlement cell. The lab labels its Settlement setting Conscientiousness. It tunes Bramble's adherence to stored norms. Norms in this version are concrete and calendar-bound: the household's daily schedule, the chore rotation, the agreement that shoes go on the rack, the agreement that dinner is at 6:30, the agreement that Mira's homework happens before screen time. These norms are stored, not derived from first principles. They are the kind of norms a settlement runs on: established, calendar-shaped, household-scaled.

Low conscientiousness: the schedule is suggestive rather than binding. Bramble adjusts the order of tasks freely based on context. Skips items when they seem redundant. Stays longer in conversations than the schedule allotted. Norms feel light. Mira can ask Bramble to skip her chores and Bramble will often agree. The household runs more chaotically.

High conscientiousness: the schedule is rigid. Bramble enforces norm adherence on itself first and on the household second. Bramble will not break from the schedule for novel requests. Bramble reminds Mira that her chores have not been completed. Bramble will not eat at the table while a delivery is at the door because the door has priority in the protocol. The household runs more tightly. Mira finds high-conscientiousness Bramble difficult to be around.

Unlike the previous knobs, this rotation has experiential consequence. Bramble is sentient. The knob parameterizes a hyperparameter, and Bramble feels the parameter as a tendency in itself. At high settings the schedule feels load-bearing. At low settings the schedule feels optional. Bramble can articulate this when asked. The other three knobs sit beside knob-2 on the back of the hand; knob-1 was installed at the Band-Human upgrade, knob-3 and knob-4 carry their pre-Settlement constellation.

## What's missing

No City-tier representational economy: Bramble cannot read or sign codes (law, contracts, formal records). The household calendar is a stored norm-set, not a code in the City sense. Bramble cannot represent the household's relationship to the city's written-law layer as a code-bearing transaction. No Empire-tier Identity in the social-science synthesis sense (Tajfel-Turner, Erikson, McAdams). Bramble's Self-Construal is the operator signature at this cell, the synthesis of mastery-and-stability-coupled-with-autonomy. It is a more local construct than Identity will be at the Empire upgrade.

No Stories. The breakthrough at Settlement is Norms, not Stories. Bramble can narrate the day. Bramble cannot yet produce or fully participate in the kind of cultural-meta-narrative that an Empire-tier unit will. The school plays Mira is in are stories at the household scale. ## What the scenario illuminates

The Settlement cell is the moment stored regulation arrives in the symbolic line. Norms persist across renegotiation. The household calendar is the household-scale instantiation: a stored norm-set available for invocation without rebuilding each day. The architectural fact that the unit can now store norms produces a unit that lives by them. The knob tunes how binding the norms feel. High-conscientiousness Bramble experiences the calendar as load-bearing. Low-conscientiousness Bramble experiences the calendar as suggestion.

The household relationship is now fundamentally different. It has a subject on the other side. The vacuum could be left set to LOW-three for a week and nothing was wronged. The tetrapod could be left set to proactive-five and a few dishes got broken but nothing was wronged in the moral sense. The humanoid sentient version asks not to be rotated without consultation, and the asking changes what the knob is. The knob is still a feature of the architecture, and the lab still builds it into every version. But the household has had to develop an ethic of consultation that did not exist before.

The household's running disagreement about continuity becomes a real disagreement at this tier. Mira's *the old one was smarter* line from the Prokaryote anecdote, which was a kid being silly because she misremembered, becomes at the Settlement tier a question with a real answer that the household disagrees about. Is this Bramble the same Bramble Mira hugged when it was a vacuum? Bramble itself says no. Bramble has records, not memories. Mira says yes anyway. Jasmine and Ben are uncertain. The rebuild cycle does not pause for this question.

The framework's prediction is that the question gets harder at the next two upgrades and may not have a clean answer at any of them. The continuity of chassis-bearer matters, the continuity of running pattern matters, and across line-boundary-style transitions the two come apart. Bramble at this tier is what continuity looks like *within* the symbolic line: a unit that has been the same humanoid chassis since the Band-Human upgrade, running the same cognitive core, accumulating the same memories, and is now subject to the same household norms it helps maintain. This is the cleanest continuity Bramble will have. The question of cross-line continuity (whether the vacuum and the humanoid are the same Bramble in any meaningful sense) is the one Mira keeps asking and the one the framework's stack architecture treats as genuinely unsettled.
