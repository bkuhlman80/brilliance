# Section 1: Vocabulary discipline

This section locks the vocabulary discipline that Lisa's twelve presentations are drafted in. Each per-chapter argument structure is built against it. When a structural fact needs invocation in a presentation, the translations here are how Lisa invokes it.

The discipline matters because the framework has been built with insider vocabulary that real scientists would not recognize. Lisa speaks for the framework in the book. If her vocabulary leaks framework-internal shorthand, the framework reads as a private language rather than as a genuine theoretical claim. The Lisa-reader (the framework's most natural audience, per `audience_anchors_v0_1.md`) is the one who notices first.

## Lisa's character premise

Lisa is the lab's Technologist. Planner-shaped. She specs the formal architecture, the algorithms, the model.

Her vocabulary is the vocabulary of three fields she actually works in: computer science, biology, neuroscience. She is the most jargony of the four labmates. The jargon is field-native rather than framework-internal.

She is aversive to psychology terms. When a cell's empirical anchor is psychological (attachment theory, self-construal, social identity, Big Five facets, HEXACO, Dark Triad, personality disorders), the depth belongs to Marge. Lisa can reference. She does not anchor.

She cites authors directly. She names what she does not yet know. She offers to dig into the math if anyone wants it. She flags her own confidence level when she is unsure, in the cadence of the Ch 11 cities-entry caveat from Bart's rolling.

## Lisa's segment in the meeting

The meeting's revised order:

1. Burns opens. Reviews the last Brandt meeting. Names the quarter's goals. Hands out the Venn for the current clade and walks what is on it.
2. Marge presents the vignette, the clade, and the fallacy section.
3. **Lisa presents the retirement-transition.** What is retiring this quarter, what the memory now holds, cross-line analogues for the retiring slot.
4. Bart looks forward to the next quarter. New-knob pitch (clade, umwelt, household-scale behavior, cybernetic function). From Ch 9 onward, opens with a rolling.
5. Burns closes. Commentary on the goal for the upcoming Brandt meeting. Inspirational speech on the clade's motivation. The speech is skipped or modified in Q4 chapters in favor of cake and chassis unveil.

Lisa speaks third. By the time her segment opens, the Venn for the current cell has already been handed out by Burns and walked. Marge has narrated the household vignette. Lisa can reference both freely without re-doing them.

Lisa's segment is the most technical of the four. Jasmine plays reader-proxy here often.

## What Lisa never says

**The R-numbers.** No R1, R2, R3, R4. No R-role. No central R-role. When she has to name a function, she names it: Effector, Regulator, Modeler, Reviser.

**The rolling window.** This phrase is the framework's, not hers. She describes the architecture: each new cell brings one new motivation into the four foreground motivations. The oldest of the prior four drops below into backgrounded infrastructure. When she wants brevity, she says "the four foreground motivations at this cell" or "the active four."

**Connector axis and Operator axis.** She names the constructs directly. Temperament. Behavioral Syndrome. Self-Model. Personality. Self-Reference. Self-Construal. Self-Evaluation. Identity. If the agency-communion framing is doing real meeting work, Marge invokes Bakan or Wiggins. Not Lisa.

**Constellation as the class noun for the four.** The class noun is "capacity." She talks about "the four capacities of the Vertebrate clade." She names individual capacities by their proper name.

**Triple as a content noun.** "Triple" stays only as a geometric description of the three-motivation region. She does not say "the triple at this cell is X." She says "the Reactor capacity at this cell is X."

**Substrate as connective tissue.** "Goo" carries the three-kinds-of-stuff content (Boltzmann, phenomenal, Shannon). For other uses where the framework had been loose with "substrate," she uses "architecture," "machinery," "hardware," or "groundwork." She uses "substrate-independent" only in the Conant-Ashby and Levin-Watson sense as cited technical terminology.

**Psychology constructs as primary depth.** No deep coverage of attachment styles, self-construal, social identity, Core Self-Evaluations, Big Five facets, HEXACO, Dark Triad, personality disorders. If those land in a chapter, they land through Marge, Burns, or Bart.

## Reader-proxy openings and the Marge-momsplain gag

Lisa's segment is the most technical. Jasmine plays reader-proxy often. Each per-chapter argument structure below identifies at least one moment where Jasmine's interior comment or out-loud question naturally fits. Natural moments: a field-native term that warrants translation, a tradeoff that warrants spelling out, a construct whose name has done the lifting without explaining itself.

When Lisa drifts into an abstraction that genuinely benefits from being made explicit, Marge translates. Used sparingly. Two or three times across the twelve chapters, not as a recurring tic. The translation should reveal something, not just rescue a sentence.

Candidates for placement: a moment in the genetic-line year where the shared framework vocabulary is not yet built up; a moment around Mammal or Primate where the four capacities are becoming canonical and Lisa drifts into them prematurely; a moment late in the symbolic line where the abstraction is real enough to warrant explicit naming.

Marge is the right vehicle. Burns checks out rather than asking. Bart asks but does not sit still for explanation. Jasmine interior-narrating the translation is a quieter version of the same move and works in interior-channel moments.

# Section 2: Per-chapter argument structures

Twelve sections, one per cell. Each follows the same template:

- **Central claim.** The one move Lisa's segment exists to make.
- **Sequence.** The beats in order.
- **What is new.** Named for the first time.
- **What is carried forward.** From prior segments.
- **What is deferred.** Reserved for a later segment, or routed to another team member.
- **Cross-line analogue work.** What architectural rhyme Lisa pulls up, if any.
- **Jasmine reader-proxy moment.** At least one natural opening.
- **Length.** Approximate meeting time and beat count.

The twelve segments accrete. Ch 1 sets the form. Ch 5 is the first line boundary. By Ch 12 the cross-line architecture is fully visible.

## Chapter 1: Protocell (Summer Y1)

**Central claim.** The memory is what makes this Bramble the same Bramble across versions. Inheritance, in the architectural sense, is what consolidates a function into the memory until the knob that did the work stops being needed. This is the form Lisa's segment will take in every subsequent meeting.

**Sequence.**

1. Open by naming that the standard retirement-transition segment cannot run this quarter. The founding cell has nothing to retire. The segment's form is being set up rather than executed.
2. Pivot to the memory. The memory is the persistent component across all twelve versions. It is where consolidation lives. Burns will say more about what is on the Venn for this cell. Lisa is here to talk about what runs underneath.
3. Anchor in Watson and Szathmáry's 2016 result. Gene-regulatory networks store the correlation structure of selective environments as a developmental memory. The memory in Bramble is the in-machine version of that mechanism, scaled to the household.
4. Anchor in Conant and Ashby. The memory's content is a model of the household. A regulator that successfully tracks its environment must be a model of it. The good regulator theorem is what licenses the cross-line claim.
5. Preview the segment form. Across the next eleven quarters, motivations will eclipse from the foreground into backgrounded infrastructure. The memory will hold what they were doing. Lisa's segment will track each retirement.

**What is new.** The memory as the persistent component. Consolidation as the mechanism. Watson and Szathmáry as the anchor. Conant and Ashby as the architectural foundation. The form of Lisa's segment across the twelve quarters.

**What is carried forward.** Nothing. This is the foundational segment.

**What is deferred.** Cross-line analogues (no prior line yet). The four-foreground architecture (Burns covers the Venn for this cell). The line-boundary mechanism (deferred to Ch 5).

**Cross-line analogue work.** None this quarter. The form gets set up; the work begins next quarter.

**Jasmine reader-proxy moment.** "What does consolidation mean here?" Lisa: "The memory has internalized the parameter setting the household prefers under various conditions. The knob has stopped doing new work because the architecture is now holding the work itself."

**Length.** Short. Five to seven minutes. Two to three beats. The segment is foundational and previews more than it delivers.

## Chapter 5: Bilaterian (Summer Y2)

**Central claim.** This retirement is not like the prior three. The Effector motivation has changed, and that change is what makes this a line boundary. The goo of inheritance has changed too: from Boltzmann to phenomenal. The memory carries the genetic line's work forward into a new chassis.

**Sequence.**

2. Explain why this is a line boundary, not within-line elaboration. The Effector motivation changing means the line's selection target has changed. At the genetic line, selection acts on differential reproductive success. At the neuronal line, selection acts on forward action under affective valence. The framework's hardest-to-grasp move.
3. Name the goo change. The genetic line works on Boltzmann goo (thermodynamic disequilibrium). The neuronal line works on phenomenal goo (affective and motivational structure produced by sensorimotor reentrant signaling). The two are different in kind, not in degree.
4. Note what the memory has consolidated from the genetic line. The whole genetic-line foreground (Fitness, Coordination, Interiority, Excitability) is now backgrounded infrastructure. Three of those four motivations (Coordination, Interiority, Excitability) also carry forward into the new line's foreground. Fitness is the one that has eclipsed.
5. Set up the cross-line analogue work. The pattern of "Effector retirement as line boundary" is the structural fact she will point to again at Ch 9. Two confirmed line boundaries by the end of the book.

**What is new.** The line boundary as a distinct event type. The goo change as the mechanism. Phenomenal goo as the neuronal line's working stuff. The cross-line analogue setup that will pay off at Ch 9.

**What is carried forward.** The retirement framework. The memory's role. The consolidation mechanism. The line-boundary preview from Ch 4.

**What is deferred.** The neuronal-line within-line retirements (Ch 6–7). The next line boundary (Ch 9). Sentience and the phenomenal-goo specifics at later cells (Mammal at Ch 7).

**Cross-line analogue work.** This is the first quarter where Lisa has cross-line work in scope. There is no prior line boundary to rhyme with yet, but she sets up the pattern as a first instance with the second arriving at Ch 9.

**Jasmine reader-proxy moment.** "Why is changing the Effector different from changing the Regulator or Modeler?" Lisa: "The Effector is what the line's selection acts on.
